Address the Issue

  • GH Feedback 3/29/16: Has this been updated for the most recent version of the AP task?
  1. Choose one issue on which you would like to focus. Do some initial research to find out: What are some technologies that address this issue?
  2. Choose one innovation to research and write about.
  3. As you research your innovation, keep track of your references. Here are the guidelines you'll have to follow for the AP Explore Task:
    • Include at least 2 and no more than 5 sources.
    • Each source must be a reliable article, book, news, or online source that anyone can access.
    • Provide the full citation with the author, title, and source. For online references, include the permanent URL and the date on which you accessed the reference.
    • At least two of the sources must have been created within the last two years.
For example, here are two sample issues.
  • Here's one way technology is being used to address an issue: using smartphones to help the homeless.
    • Fighting Homelessness, One Smartphone at a Time (by Claire Cain Miller, New York Times, 4/14/15)
    • Smartphones are a Lifeline for Homeless People (by Rosie Spinks, The Guardian, 10/1/15)
  • How has the innovation of social media affected dating?
    • Teens, Technology, and Romanic Relationships (by Amanda Lenhart, Monica Anderson, and Aaron Smith, from Pew Research Center, 10/1/15)
    • Teen Voices: Dating in the Digital Age (from Pew Research Center, 10/1/15)
  1. Describe your innovation using these two (out of four) prompts from the AP Explore Task:
    • Explain the technical details of this innovation in terms that someone completely unfamiliar with the innovation would understand.
    • Describe the role computing plays in implementing the functionality associated with the innovation.
  2. Describe the population impacted by your innovation. Use the criteria from the AP Explore Task:
    • Include population characteristics such as approximate size, socioeconomic status, geographic location, health, age, gender, ethnicity, race, sexual orientation, and disability.
  3. Describe the long-term and short-term impacts of your innovation.
