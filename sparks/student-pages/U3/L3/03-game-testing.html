Game Testing

In this activity, you'll get feedback from classmates on your game and iterate on your design.

It's important to get feedback from others who are less familiar with your game. Use the instructions you wrote up in Activity 2 and share your game with another pair of classmates and ask them for feedback.

  1. Answer the following questions after testing your classmates' game:
    • Was it easy to play?
    • Did the rules make sense?
    • Was there a clear winner?
    • What did you like about the game? What changes would you suggest your classmates make?
  2. After receiving feedback from your classmates, discuss with your partner what changes could improve your game play.
If you have completed your game improvements early, make a game of the opposite type. For example, if you made a micro:bit only game, make a game that uses additional materials or visa versa.
