Make It Move

In this activity, you'll learn about servo motors, how to connect one to your micro:bit, and use MicroBlocks to control its movement.

Collect Materials

    • micro:bit and USB cable
    • Servo motor
    • Servo support board for connecting the servo motor (such as a Crazy Circuits Bit Board or a basic:bit board)
    • Phillips head screwdriver (only needed if you have a basic:bit board)
    • Craft supplies (see Lab 2 Activity 2: Building Your Pet)

Get Started

Motors are one way to add movement to your projects. There are many different types of motors, including one called a servo motor.

: Servo Motor

A servo motor (or just servo) provides position control, so it can be told to move into an exact spot. Its position can be selected from 0 to 180 degrees.

  1. Connect your micro:bit to your servo support board and connect your servo to the servo support board:
    • Connection instructions for the Crazy Circuits Bit Board
      1. The direction you plug things in matters!
        • The LEDs on the micro:bit should be facing out when plugged into the Bit Board.
        • The orange (lightest) servo wire should be farthest from the micro:bit board and the brown (darkest) servo wire should be closest to the micro:bit board.
        • If you are using the battery pack, the red wire should be plugged in to the "+" side and the black wire should be plugged in to the "-" side.
        Insert your micro:bit into the Bit Board, pins facing down as shown in the diagram below. (As always, use the micro USB cable to connect the micro:bit to your computer.)
      2. Connect a servo to the pins on the Bit Board, making note of which servo number you connect to (0, 1, 13, 14, or 15). (The image shows two servos attached, but you only need one.)
    • Connection instructions for the basic:bit board
      1. The direction you plug things in matters!
        • The LEDs on the micro:bit should be facing out and the basic:bit logo should be facing out.
        • The orange (lightest) servo wire should be connected to the numbered pin (0, 1, or 2) and the brown (darkest) servo wire should be connected to the ground (G) pin.
        • If you are using the battery pack, the red wire should be plugged in to the "+" side as usual.
        Use the screws provided to connect your basic:bit board to the micro:bit as shown in the diagram below. (As always, use the micro USB cable to connect the micro:bit to your computer.)
      2. Connect a servo to the pins on the basic:bit board, making note of which servo number you connect to (0, 1, or 2).
  2. Add the servo library in MicroBlocks by pressing "Add Library," selecting "Servo" (toward the bottom of the list), and pressing "Open."

    You should see a "Servo" block menu appear at the bottom of the palette on the left of the MicroBlocks screen (as shown here).

Add Movement

You can control the servo motor by using the servo motor blocks. After adding the servo library, you'll have the following blocks available:

  • set servo to degrees moves the servo arm to a particular position in degrees. The range of -90 to 90 degrees is equivalent to 180 degrees. Be sure to address the right servo (check your Bit Board to see which port your servo is connected to, and use the chart as reference for determining servo position in degrees).
  • set servo to speed determines the speed at which the servo moves. The speed range is from 0 to 100, and the sign (positive or negative) determines the direction it turns.
  • stop servo stops the servo's movements.
    • Each block asks to specify the servo number. You must use the number shown near the pins that your servo is connected to.
