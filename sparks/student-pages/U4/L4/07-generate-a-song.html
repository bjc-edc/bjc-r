You used a similar block in the Song Player project in Unit 2 Lab 1.

For stories, our tokens were words. For songs, our token will be a note. There are different ways computers can store notes as data. In Snap!, we use this block . Each note is defined with two numbers: the pitch (how high or low it sounds) and the length (how long to play it). For those who know musical notes, a pitch of 60 corresponds to middle C. The default tempo is 1 beat per second, so 0.5 beats is half a second.

The N-gram project we've been using contains a dataset of 20 children's songs. If you click on you will see a list of 943 notes, each with a pitch and a length. The first 21 notes are shown here. For those who are curious, these notes are the first part of Twinkle, Twinkle, Little Star.

  2. Describe the first three 3-grams.

You can play all the songs by using this block. Tip: If you want it to play faster, look for a block (in the Sound palette) that sets the tempo and change the number of beats per minute (bpm).
