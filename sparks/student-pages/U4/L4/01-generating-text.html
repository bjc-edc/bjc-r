Generating Text

In this activity, you'll think of how to approach the task of generating text.

Can you sometimes tell from a short text message which of your friends wrote that text? Do you think you would be able tell whether a quote came from Shakespeare or Taylor Swift? If you said "yes," you're probably thinking of certain patterns in the way your friends or well-known artists use language, though it might hard to say exactly what those patterns are.

Being able to generate text that sounds "natural" (like a human, and not like a machine) has been a problem that many AI researchers have worked on for a very long time.

  1. Pick one of your favorite authors or songwriters. Describe to your partner their writing style. What are some features that makes their style special?
  2. Now imagine putting all the words to all the books/poems/songs written by your favorite author or songwriter into a computer. How could a computer use that data (that is, all those words) to generate a new book or song in the style of your favorite artist without copying their work completely?
  3. Is it a good idea to use your favorite author or songwriter's work in this way? Who is impacted by this decision?
  4. Have you ever read something and wondered if it was computer-generated rather than human-generated? What are some features that makes text "feel" computer-generated?

Generative AI, or GenAI, produces new text, images, music, based on patterns in a (usually very large) set of data.

In this activity, you started to appreciate what it might take to generate text.
