Training Your ML Model

In this activity, you'll train a machine learning model to recognize the image classes needed for your project.

In machine learning, a model is like a recipe for detecting patterns in data. A classification model sorts data into classes (like "spam" vs. "not spam," or "cat" vs. "dog").

  1. Open Google's Teachable Machine.
  2. Click “Get started,” then “Image Project,” and then "Standard image model."
  3. Rename "Class 1" and "Class 2" to describe your classes, add and rename additional classes if needed, and gather the things you plan to show the camera to create each class.
  4. For each class:

    1. You might need to give your browser permission to access the webcam.

      Click "Webcam," place the item in front of the camera, and press "Hold to Record" to gather some images.
    2. Sometimes things look different and the computer still needs to be able to classify them correctly. Mix it up. For example: Record some images with the object rotated or showing a different side. Maybe a person could make a different kind of face. Maybe you can point your hand a different way.
    3. Get at least 100 image samples for each class.
    4. Delete any images that accidentally get recorded but don't match the class well.
    5. Repeat for each of your classes.
  5. Click "Train model." Do not switch tabs while the model is being trained. If you see the message "page is unresponsive," click "wait."
    The model takes time to train, and your browser might think the website has crashed. You'll have to be patient for a minute to two.
  6. If a pop-up tells you to export the model and blocks the "Preview" window, close the pop-up. You can export later.
    Use the "Preview" window to test your model. Add or edit your image samples and re-train the model as necessary so that it usually guess the right class as output for whatever show the camera as input.
  7. Discuss:
    1. Review your plan. What do you want your project to do with each image?
    2. Consider your model. Do you have enough classes for your plan?
    3. Do you want other people to use your program? Would it help to train your model with other people or backgrounds?
  8. Improve and re-train the model as needed.
  9. When it works well, click "Export model," select the "Upload (shareable link)" option, and click "Update my cloud model." The shareable link should turn blue, and you can “Copy” the link.
  10. Discuss with other students:
    1. What issues did you run into?
    2. How did you improve your model?
In this activity, you trained a machine learning model to classify images. Next, you'll import the model into Snap! and use the camera to control your project.
