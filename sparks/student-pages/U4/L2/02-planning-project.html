Planning Your Project

In this activity, you'll plan a Snap! project that will be controlled by what you do or put in front of your computer camera.

In machine learning, a class is like a category or a bucket that the computer learns to sort things into. For example, you could train a computer to understand the difference between two classes of images: cats and dogs by showing it lots of images of both animals and telling it which animal each picture is.

Your project will need two or more different classes of images for the camera to see and for your project to recognize and respond to. For example, the project shown below recognizes three different classes of images:

  1. Images where the person is pointing up
  2. Images where the person is pointing to the right
  3. Images where the person isn't pointing at all
The video below shows how the program responds to these three different classes of images detected by the camera. When program detects an image classified as "up," the sprite moves up; when it detects "right," it moves right; and when it doesn't detect either, it returns to the lower left corner

  1. Click the image above to watch the person control the sprite with their hand.
  2. Think of a simple Snap! project that would be controlled by something in front of the camera, and write down 2-4 different classes of images. Here are some examples:
    • Hand pointing up, pointing right, no hand
    • Awake vs. sleeping
    • Different people: you, a friend, no one
    • Expressions: neutral, smiling, frowning
    • Different objects
    • Different colors
  3. Write down what your project will do when the computer detects each of your classes.
