Planning Your Project

In this activity, you'll plan a Snap! project that will be controlled by what you do or put in front of your computer camera.

In machine learning, a class is like a category or a bucket that the computer learns to sort things into. For example, you could train a computer to understand the difference between images of cats and images of dogs by showing it lots of images of both animals and telling it which animal each picture is. That project would have two classes: cats and dogs.

Your project will need two or more different classes of images for the camera to see and for your project to recognize and respond to. The example project you saw recognizes three different classes of images:

  1. Images where the person is pointing up
  2. Images where the person is pointing to the right
  3. Images where the person isn't pointing at all
Here's the video again so you can see how the program responds to these three different classes of images detected by the camera. When the program detects an image classified as "up," the sprite moves up; when it detects "right," it moves right; and when it doesn't detect either, it is supposed to return to the lower left corner.

  1. Think of a simple Snap! project that would be controlled by something in front of the camera, and write down 2-4 different classes of images. Here are some examples:
    • Hand pointing up, pointing right, no hand
    • Awake vs. sleeping
    • Different people: you, a friend, no one
    • Expressions: neutral, smiling, frowning
    • Rock, paper, scissors
    • Different objects (like an apple and banana)
    • Different pictures
    • Different colors
  2. Write down what your project will do when the computer detects each of your classes.
In this activity, you learned about image classes and made up classes and decided how they will control a Snap! project.
