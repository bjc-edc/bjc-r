In this activity, you'll try categorizing images like a computer does—from just a bunch of pixels.

You learned about pixels in Unit 1 Lab 4 Activity 1: Picture of a Pixel.
Computers don't see the same way people do. Computers just "see" a bunch of pixels.

  1. Thanks to Zak Kolar, a teacher in Waltham, MA for sharing this project with BJC Sparks!
    Open this Image Classifier Demo. Each image is a recognizable object with its pixels rearranged so you can't tell what it is. There are two "classes" (or types) of images.
  2. Several "Examples" of each class are given to you. Sort the "Test Data" in the middle into the “Guesses” sections under each class by dragging and dropping the images.
  3. When you think you've classified the images correctly, click the "Check" button to see the original images unscrambled.
    1. Were the examples good enough to classify the new (test) data? Why or why not?
    2. What do you think "CLass 1" and "Class 2" were intended to be? What did "Class 1" and "Class 2" end up being?
    3. How would you improve the examples?
    4. In this demo, images were only classified by pixel color. What other image characteristics would be helpful to know?
    5. Even with the additional examples and additional characteristics you identified, would it still be possible to "fool" your image classifier? How?
