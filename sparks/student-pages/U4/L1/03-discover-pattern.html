In this activity, you'll start thinking about how a computer can develop a coin game strategy.

One way to train a computer to play is to have it consider how a player's current position (the number of coins left) impacts whether they can win (if both players play perfectly). If there is 1 coin left, a player will always win, and if there are 2, a perfect player will always win. We call situations like these "winning positions."

Now consider 3 coins left. If you take 1, your opponent can win by taking 2, but if you take 2, they will take 1 and always win. We call states like these "losing positions."

  1. What if there were 4 coins left? Is that a winning position or a losing position?
  2. What are the winning positions? What are the losing positions? Document which positions win and which positions lose.
