Discovering the Win/Lose Pattern

In this activity, you'll start thinking about how a computer can develop a coin game strategy.

One way to train a computer to play is to have it consider how the state of the game (the number of coins left) impacts who can win (assuming both players play perfectly). Obviously, if there is 1 coin left on your turn, you will always win, and if there are 2, you can win if you play perfectly by taking both. We will call situations like these "winning states."

A state is a description of what's currently true about the game.

Imagine there are 3 coins left on your turn. If you take 1, they can win by taking 2, but if you take 2, they will always win. We will call states like these "losing states."

  1. What if there are 4 coins left on your turn? Is that a winning state or a losing state?
  2. What are the winning states? What are the losing states?
  3. Document which states let a perfect player always win and in which states even a perfect player can lose.
