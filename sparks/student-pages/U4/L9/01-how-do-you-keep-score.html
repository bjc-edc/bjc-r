In previous labs, we have seen examples of reinforcement learning in action. In the coins game, you saw an algorithm develop over multiple matches. At first, the algorithm used by the machine was completely random. But as you played and the machine was able to learn about the game through the reward system, it made smarter and smarter decisions. In hexapawn, the in-class activity showed you clearly how the reward system could influence the moves a machine could make. Bad moves that led to a losing state were punished, while good moves that led to a winning state were rewarded. As worlds get more complex, how do we keep track of the score? In the coin game, it was simple enough to keep track of all the different game states. Hexapawn could be tracked with N board states and 2 moves.
  1. Discuss how does hexapawn or the coin game keep track of whether or not a move is good or bad?
  2. What differences were there between hexapawn and the coin game?
  3. What parts between hexapawn and the coin game stayed the same?
