A key part of reinforcement learning is how an agent learns from its environment. In Q-learning, enough exploration has to be done so that each move learns the optimal Q-value. Previously in hexapawn, you were responsible for helping the agent explore by playing the game. But what if you wanted the agent to explore the world on its own?

An exploration policy can be used by an agent to explore the world on its own. However different exploration policies can lead to different results. There could be a tradeoff between training time, speed to find an optimal solution, or failure rate depending on the exploration policy you select. Let's look at some different policy examples.

Random Exploration Policy - Agent chooses a move randomly. Every move at each state has equal chance of being selected.

Exploitative Exploration Policy - Agent uses information it has learned to choose the best path. This policy starts out like the random exploration policy, but once q values are updated for a given state-move pair, it will use that information to maximize reward whenever possible. Some randomness is still needed to ensure agent explores properly.

Other policies - There are many other policies that can be used to explore. Polcies can be created to adjust for real life resource constraints, to be based on assumptions about the environment, or any number of reasons.

Talk with your partner and talk about what are the pros and cons of a random or exploitative policy. Discuss what situations you could use either policy in, or what you could do to make each policy better.
