    Sound Waves

    Sound is created by pressure waves traveling through a material (like air or water). When you hear music, an instrument or speaker (think of a vibrating guitar string, a vibrating drum head, or a vibrating speaker) creates waves of pressure that travel through the air to your eardrum, which transmits the signal through your nerves to your brain. Have you ever heard a sound that was so loud you could feel it? Those were pressure waves that were so big you could sense them with more than just your eardrum!

      Testing a Microphone in Snap!

      1. Click the "Sounds..." tab above the scripting area.
      2. The browser may ask you for permission to access the microphone. Click "Allow."
        Click the record button () to open the "Sound Recorder."
      3. Click the record button in the "Sound Recorder" (the circle), make some sounds (talk, laugh, etc.), and press stop (the square) when you are done.
      4. Click the play button (the triangle) to check that the microphone recorded your sound.
        5. If you aren't able to record a sound, check the audio settings in the computer's system settings/preferences. If you can't figure it out, ask your teacher.
      5. Click "Cancel" since you are just testing the microphone.

    3. Explore the microphone block. Drag it out of the "Sensing" palette, and click it several times for each of the following inputs:

      3. reports the number of values in a microphone (samples) list
