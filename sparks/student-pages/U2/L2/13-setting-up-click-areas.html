Setting Up Your Click Areas

In this activity, you will create a predicate expression to check whether the mouse is in your click area(s).

A predicate is a hexagon-shaped reporter that asks a true/false question.

You learned about predicates in Unit 1 Lab 6 Activity 2: L33t Text.

When the user clicks the stage, your program should check whether the mouse is in any of your click areas. You can use and from the "Sensing" palette to check.

The following example predicate expression checks if the x position of the mouse is between -80 and -240 and also if the the y position is between 180 and 60. That is, it tests whether the mouse is in the yellow section of the stage shown in the picture of the stage shown below.

Whenever the user clicks the stage, you can use an predicate expression like this to test whether they clicked in one of your click areas and use the result to decide whether to perform an action (such as an animation or sound).
