Social Implications Lab 5: Censorship and Computing around the World

This lab takes the broad structure and content of Blown to Bits Chapter 7. Students first consider what rules (if any) ought to exist on the Internet and then learn of some of the practical difficulties of regulating Internet content. Students then consider some issues of free speech and are confronted with the fact that a global Internet leads to issues when different countries have different standards for what should be censored. Finally, students take this opportunity to examine some statistics for Internet use around the world.

Beware that many potential sensitive topics are addressed on these pages. The first story in the Blown to Bits reading is about a romantic connection online. While dramatic, this particular story did not have a devastating end, but it is possible that students may have experienced stalking or worse online. Chapter 7 in Blown to Bits examines censorship primarily in terms of how pornographic materials are treated online. The students are not assigned reading from most of these sections, but students may read these sections and have questions or raise issues. In materials about Internet censorship, specific countries are identified as having particularly harsh censorship policies. The United States is not immune from this, but be aware of statements that are made that appear to judge people of other nationalities. Part of the point of thinking through the social implications of these issues is to realize that these issues are not simple, and that different policies reflect different ways that societies have grappled with difficult and complex issues.
