Lab 5: Secure Communication

Students learn about various cybersecurity risks on the Internet (including the fundamental lack of DNS security and common security attacks) and the basic concepts of cryptography.

Pacing:

The 2 lab pages could be split across 1 or 2 days (50-80 minutes). Expected times to complete follow:
  • 10-20 minutes (<1 class period) on Cybersecurity
  • 40-60 minutes (about 1 class period) on Cryptography

Students will:

  • Learn about cybersecurity, cyber warfare, and cyber crime, including distributed denial-of-service attacks, phishing, viruses, and antivirus software.
  • Learn about cryptography, including symmetric and public key encryption, one-way functions, and certificate authorities.
  • Develop a simple encryption script.

Goals for the Lab:

  • Page 1: Cybersecurity.
    • Understand DNS redirects.
    • Understand the risks of computer viruses and typical mechanisms for infection, including unintentional installation and security vulnerabilities.
    • Understand phishing.
    • Understand DoS and DDoS attacks.
  • Page 2: Cryptography.
    • Understand the basic protocol behind symmetric and public key encryption.

As a Class:

  • Page 1: Cybersecurity.
    • Discussion: This discussion covers the third essential question for Big Idea 6: The Internet in the AP CS Frameworks.
      • How is cybersecurity impacting the ever-increasing number of Internet users?
  • Page 2: Cryptography.
    • Discussion: How does asymmetric encryption work? Why is it called asymmetric?

Assignments:

  • Assessment Idea:
    • Describe a security attack and how cryptography helps protect against the attack.
