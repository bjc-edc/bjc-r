Programming Lab 1: Building an App

Students go right into working with snap in the very first class. Consider giving students 30-40 minutes to explore the lab in pairs before taking care of mandatory first-day business. In Lab 1, students set up their snap accounts for saving projects and build and share a simple game.

As the year begins, it is important that students know what is expected of them. Labs are intended to be times during which students work at their own pace in pairs. They should know that they are responsible for getting through the material in the section, but that they are also free to explore and create if something of interest to them. Expect glitches, and also model the necessary patience and perseverance necessary to work with new technology.

Pacing

The 7 lab pages could be split across one or two days. Expected times to complete follow:

  • 15-20 minutes on Creating a Snap! Account
  • 5-10 minutes on Starting Your First Snap! App
  • approx. 1 minute on Pair Programming Swap
  • 5-10 minutes on Using the Random Block
  • approx. 5 minutes on Saving in Snap!
  • 10-15 minutes on Finishing Your First Snap! App
  • approx. 5 minutes on Sharing Your App and Loading It on a Mobile Device

Prepare:

  • Read the Teacher Guide Introduction and this Unit 1 Programming Lab 1 Teacher Guide.
  • Review the Unit 1 Student Programming Lab 1, Building an App.
  • Prepare several Openers.
  • Prepare to support students with managing their Snap! projects:
    • Creating a Snap! Account and Logging in
    • Saving and Opening Projects
    • Sharing Your App with a Partner and Loading Your App on a Mobile Device

As a Class:

  • Work through the Programming Lab 1, Building an App and the relevant Week 1 Activities.
  • Consider working through the Week 1 Assignments that you don't assign as homework together as a class.

Tips:

  • In the Using the Random Block lab, students must "Add a line of code so that every time Alonzo is clicked he not only turns, but also moves to a random position (between -190 and 190 in the x direction and between -130 and 130 in the y direction)." This may be a challenge for some students. Suggest that students re-watch the brief animation included in the lab pages and that they consider how to code the desired x-position (a random number between -190 and 190) and desired y-position (a random number between -130 and 130) separately. Encourage students to break down complex ideas.
