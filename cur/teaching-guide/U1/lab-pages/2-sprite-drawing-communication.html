Programming Lab 2: Sprite Drawing and Communication

In Lab 2, students explore block programming with sprites. Students write a simple script to draw a square and then modify the input values and discuss the results. They work with multiples, creating scripts in which sprites follow each other or follow the mouse around the stage. And they code with more complicated programming structures such as loops and conditionals.

In this lab, students deepen their experience with loops and conditionals still without formalizing. Resist the urge to "teach" these concepts and instead allow time for students to explore and discover how things work on their own. Lab 3: Control Commands codifies this learning.

Pacing

The 5 lab pages could be split across two or three days. Expected times to complete follow:

  • 15-20 minutes on Exploring Snap Drawing and Motion
  • 10-15 minutes on Following with Sprites
  • 20-25 minutes on Sprites Interaction
  • 15-20 minutes on Drawing with Two Sprites
  • 5-10 minutes on More Sprite Drawing and Interaction

Reminders appear in the following Unit 1 lab pages: Sprite Interaction and The For Block.
  • The first page of this lab gives students a simple script to draw a square and asks them to experiment with it, changing various numbers in the script and observing the results. Students who are accustomed to being told exactly what to do may flounder; remind them that Creativity is one of the CSP Big Ideas, and that there are no wrong answers to this sort of exploratory lesson.
  • Take note of any interesting approaches or creative ideas that would be interesting to share. Consider also asking students to submit yet-incomplete projects (giving them some way of indicating this in the file name) if you see an interesting intermediate attempt that could spark discussion.
