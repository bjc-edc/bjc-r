Programming Lab 5: Block Types and Calculations

Students continue experimenting with and combining blocks as they formalize their understanding of the Snap!intterface (sprite position and direction) and blocks. Students learn to differentiate between command blocks (including loops and conditionals), reporter blocks (and watchers), and predicates (including Boolean constant blocks). After most of a unit exploring a variety of blocks, students now learn how block shapes relate to their functionality and how the different types of blocks tend to relate (reporters inside commands and other reporters, Booleans inside predicates, predicates inside predicates, predicates inside conditionals, etc.).

Students also explore a variety of math reporters (from the Operators palette) including basic operations, round, mod, and logical operators (true, false, and, or, and not) and refine their understanding of the random block as they refine their understanding of block relationships.

Pacing

The 4 lab pages could be split across one or two days (35-55 minutes). Expected times to complete follow:

  • 5-10 minutes on Sprite Position and Direction
  • 10-15 minutes on Expressions and Operations
  • 10-15 minutes on Predicates and Logic
  • 10-15 minutes on Random Block Self Check

Prepare:

  • Prepare several Computing in the News stories.

Students will:

  • Connect the idea of sprite position and direction in Snap! to the conventions for referencing position and direction in mathematics.
  • Formalize their language and understanding of several different types of blocks in snap.
  • Broaden and refine their understanding of the mathematics of computer science including basic arithmetic operations, the round block, the mod block, the random block, and logical operators.
  • Analyze code with nested operations.

Discussion/Writing Prompts 

  • Types of Blocks:
    • What kinds of blocks have you learned about so far?
    • How are each kind of block visually distinguished from the others?
    • What is an example of using a reporter within a command block?
    • What is an example of using a predicate within a conditional?
  • Mathematics of Computing:
    • What does the random block do? How does it work?
    • What does the mod block do? How does it work?
    • What are some programs or tasks that would benefit from using the mod block?
    • What is the difference between or and and?
    • What does the not block do?
