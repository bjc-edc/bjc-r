Programming Lab 1: Introduction

Students go right into working with snap in the very first class. As you take care of mandatory first-day business, make sure to leave students 30-40 minutes to explore the hour of code projects in pairs. In Lab 1, students explore block programming with a simple script to draw a square, set up their snap accounts for saving projects, build a video game, and get to know the snap stage as a coordinate plane.

As the year begins, it is important that students know what is expected of them. Labs are intended to be times during which students work at their own pace in pairs. They should know that they are responsible for getting through the material in the section, but that they are also free to explore and create if something of interest to them. Occasionally, there are prompts in the labs directing students to post their work, whether it be to a Google classroom feed, Piazza, or some other platform. Allow time for the students (and yourself) to get used to the technology. Expect glitches, and also model the necessary patience and perseverance necessary to work with new technology.

Pacing

The 5 lab pages could be split across two or three days. Expected times to complete follow:

  • 10-15 minutes on snap Accounts and Files
  • 10-15 minutes on Looks and Sound
  • 10-15 minutes on Sprite Position and Direction
  • 10-15 minutes on Getting to Know the snap Interface
