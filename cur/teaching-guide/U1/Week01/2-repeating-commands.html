Programming Lab 2: Control Commands

Students use loops to repeat similar steps of code. Students compare and contrast functionally equivalent scripts (one that uses a loop and one without) to apprecaite the clarity, simplicity, and elegance of looping. Students consider the implications of nesting repeat blocks within repeat blocks and explore short scripts with forever and for blocks.

Nesting repeat blocks within repeat blocks previews Building Blocks within Blocks in Lab 3. Students develop understanding of abstraction as they see how projects can be broken down into smaller tasks and programmed in chunks. This practice helps the programmer conceptualise the project and makes the code easier to read and debug. Recursion (calling a procedure from within itself) will be addressed in much greater detail in Unit 5: Recursion and Unit 6: Recursive Reporters.

Pacing

The 4 lab pages could be split across two or three days. Expected times to complete follow:

  • 15-20 minutes on The Repeat Block
  • 10-15 minutes on Nesting Repeats
  • 10-15 minutes on The Forever Block
  • 15-20 minutes on The For Block

A common misunderstanding about drawing polygons for all new Snap! students is to confuse the interior angles of the figure with the angles of sprite rotation. These are not generally the same, and are, in fact, supplementary. To clarify the distinction, have students draw sketches with marked angles and discuss why the term "supplementary" is appropriate.
