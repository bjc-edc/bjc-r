Optional assignments for class discussion, silent work, or homework (for use anytime during or after the indicated lab):

  • Writing Prompts:
      If you assign this writing prompt, collect and scan/photocopy the responses to use at the very beginning of Lab 6.
    • How do you interact with computers and digital media? This initial writing assignment can help you gauge both students' writing abilities and familiarity with computers. This is also their first assignment on Google docs, so you can go over the mechanics of accessing their assignment. This assignment can also be completed offline for those without the necessary access at home. (Lab 1)
    • Consider assigning some of the Week 1 discussion prompts as writen assignments.

You may wish to specifically assign one or more of the optional "Take it Further" problems:

  • Lab 1, Pair Programming Swap: Reading How Pair Programming Really Works by Stuart Wray
  • Lab 1, Finishing Your First Snap! App: Adding code to the app so that Alonzo moves randomly
  • Lab 2, Exploring snap Drawing and Motion:
    • Creating a drawing script
    • Drawing a circle with many tiny turns
    • Drawing a circle red inside of a blue square
  • Lab 2, Sprite Interaction:
    • Resizing sprite costumes
    • Making a Sprite Dialogue
    • Adding a third sprite
    • Chanving the background on the stage
  • Lab 2, Drawing with Two Sprites:
    • Several extended experiments with different sprite turns
    • Creating interesting designs
    • WRiting out the results of these experiments
  • Lab 3, The Real Value of the Repeat Block:
    • Investigating polygons with more sides (triangle, pentagon, hexagon, octagon)
    • Determining turning angles for 12-sided and 36-sided regular polygons
    • Determining the total degrees in drawing a regular 9-pointed star
    • Drawing a regular 5-pointed star
    • Determining the total degrees in drawing the regular 5-pointed star
    • Orienting the star
  • Lab 3, Nesting Repeats:
    • Adding a fourth level of triangles to the Sierpinski fractal
  • Lab 3, The For Block:
    • Counting backwards from 100 by tens
    • Nesting squares
    • Nesting polygons
    • Creating musical scripts
    • Counting by hundredths
