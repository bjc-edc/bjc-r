Prepare

  • Make your own snap account using your classroom's technology
  • Think about and set up other technology (Google classroom, Piazza, etc.) you'd like students to use
  • Read this article about pair programming. Students will also read this later.
  • Check out these tips from csteachingtips.org:
    • Have students sign a "Computer Use Agreement" before giving them access to machines to hold them accountable for using computers responsibly.
    • Encourage students to talk to each other to decrease the burden on the teacher and to emulate industry work environments.
AP CS Principles Computational Thinking Practices:
  • P6 Collaborating Collaborate with another student in solving a computational problem.
  • P6 Collaborating Exchange knowledge and feedback with a partner or team member.
AP CS Principles Essential Knowledge:
  • EK 4.1.1B Sequencing is the application of each step of an algorithm in the order in which the statements are given.
  • EK 5.1.3B Collaboration facilitates multiple perspectives in developing ideas for solving problems by programming.
  • EK 5.1.3F Effective communication between participants is required for successful collaboration when developing programs.
  • EK 5.2.1B Program instructions are executed sequentially.
