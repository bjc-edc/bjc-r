Programming Lab 4: Block Types and Calculations

Students continue experimenting with and combining blocks as they formalize their understanding of command blocks (including loops and conditionals), reporter blocks (and watchers), and predicates (including Boolean constant blocks). After most of a unit exploring a variety of blocks, students now learn how block shapes relate to their functionality and how the different types of blocks tend to relate (reporters inside commands and other reporters, Booleans inside predicates, predicates inside predicates, predicates inside conditionals, etc.). Students also explore a variety of math reporters (from the Operators palette) including basic operations, random, mod, and logical operators (true, false, and, or, and not) as they refine their understanding of block relationships.

Pacing

The 3 lab pages could be split across two days. Expected times to complete follow:

  • 20-25 minutes on Commands and Reporters
  • 15-20 minutes on Predicates, Conditionals, and Logic
  • 20-25 minutes on Operators

Prepare:

  • Review the Unit 1 Student Programming Lab 4.
  • Prepare a Computing in the News story.
  • Prepare several other Openers.

Students will:

  • Formalize their language and understanding of several different types of blocks in Snap!.
  • Explore the mathematics of computer science including basic arithmetic operations, the random block, the mod block, and logical operators.
  • Analyze code with nested operations.

As a Class:

  • Work through the Programming Lab 4: Block Types and Calculations and the Week 1 Activities and Assignments.

Tips:

  • Remind students that they can run scripts by clicking on them, including reporters, which will report their result in a speech balloon next to the block.
