Lab 3: Turning Data into Information

Page still needs major edits to match student pages. --MF, 6/26/19

This lab focuses on the remaining content of AP CSP Big Idea 3: Data. Students use existing online data visualization tools to identifying patterns in data and work collaboratively to draw conclusions and summarize their findings. Several AP-style self-check questions are included to help students extend their understanding to the type of questions likely to appear on the exam.

Pacing

The 6 lab pages could be split across X-X days (X-X minutes). Expected times to complete follow:
  • 25-50 minutes (about 1 class period) on Analyzing Global Health Data
  • 10-20 minutes (<1 class period) on Self-Check: Big Data
  • X-X minutes (X class period) on Importing and Accessing Data
  • X-X minutes (X class period) on Analyzing Data
  • X-X minutes (X class period) on Visualizing Data
  • 10-30 minutes (<1 class period) on Metadata

    • Tips:
      • Figuring out how to report just one column of a table can be tricky for students. They'll need to map item of over their dataset. For example, reports just the MPG of every car in the CORGIS Cars CSV Library. The map function is simple to understand (e.g, add 3 to every item in the list, or take the fourth item of every record in the table), but it can take time and practice to learn when and how to use it. Encourage students to take that time by talking with their partner, referring back to Unit 3 Lab 1 Page 4: Transforming Every List Item where they first learned map, and/or talking to other pairs.
      • After students design and answer their own questions, having them present their questions and solutions to the class will help everyone better appreciate the power of using higher order functions (such as map and keep) to process data and better understand how to use them with a list of lists.
