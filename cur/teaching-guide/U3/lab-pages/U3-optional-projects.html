Lab Pages

  • Page 1: Curve Stitching.
    • Learning Goals:
      • Apply skills with developing algorithms and using loops in the context of a visual arts project.
    • Tips:
      • Students can approach this project in two different ways: one is to fix the Cartesian coordinates of the virtual strings; and the other is to use a turtle geometry approach in which motions are relative. The initial task for students is probably easier with fixed coordinates, but the Take It Further A, if the students start the projects with relative positions. Either approach is acceptable. The solutions provided use fixed coordinates.
      • Some students may find it difficult to generalize the algorithm they developed to generate the first quadrant of the design and apply it to other quadrants. The first quadrant involves joining positive y-axis points to positive x-axis points, while the second quadrant involves joining positive y-axis points to negative x-axis points, etc. You might help students see this by asking them to draw the design on graph paper and label the connection points.

  • Page 4: Music Project.
    Project and TG need BH review. --MF, 9/9/18
    • Learning Goals:
        Cut. --MF, 9/10/18
      • Explore computational methods of creative expression through music.
      • Review abstract data types and higher order functions in a musical context.
      • Create a computational environment for musical expression.
    • Tips:
      • Students will need headphones or speakers to work on the Music Project. Headphones are recommended to reduce distraction to other students. Pairs may appreciate having two sets of headphones and a headphone splitter, which costs about $5-$10, if working on a computer without two audio output ports.
      • Students familiar with music may want to use fractions (e.g. 0.25, 0.5, etc.) to represent note lengths (e.g. quarter notes, half notes, etc.) and adjust the tempo accordingly. Integer note lengths are offered on the lab pages for students unfamiliar with the nuances of representing musical time signatures as decimals (e.g. 0.25 + 0.5 being a full measure in 3/4 time, but not 4/4 time). Integers work just as well and are more forgiving.
      • Consider inviting students with musical training to present basic music theory (especially beat length representation) and to share and describe their music script(s) with the class.
Modeling Sentence Structures Stuff from Y2U3L1:
  • Goals for the Lab: Page 3: Modeling Sentence Structures.
    • Use the join and join words blocks.
    • Algorithmically select from lists.
    • Randomly choose items from lists of words to make a Sentence Builder.
  • As a Class: Page 3: Modeling Sentence Structures.
    • The page is designed to offer a fun mini-project in which the use of lists feels natural and empowering. Encourage students to be creative and build other kinds of sentences or even simple conversations.
    • This lesson relies on proficiency in programming more than knowledge of English sentence structure. For this kind of project, English is particularly easy because it has hardly any inflections (case, tense, mood, etc.), but students may try this project in a language other than English, if they like. Languages present different challenges but students can adjust their lists or their coding to make the project work. Students could work with others or alone and then share. Be creative. For example, it might be beneficial to pair a student studying Spanish with a fluent Spanish speaker.
    • As always, if students do interesting work (e.g. with another language or with the Take It Further problems), consider inviting them to share with the class.
    • Discussion, you might ask: How do the sentence builder blocks demonstrate abstraction?
  • Tips: Page 3: Modeling Sentence Structures.
    • The English grammar on this page may be too complex for some English Language Learners to stay focused on the computer science. On the other hand, English is the least inflected natural language, and even an ESL student may find the programming easier in English.
