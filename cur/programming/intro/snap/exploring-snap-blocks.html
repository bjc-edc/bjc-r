We name the sections of the snap window so that we can talk about them.

The area called the palette contains the blocks (individual instructions) that you will use to tell the computer what you want it to do. The blocks are organized into eight categories according to the kind of thing each block does.

  • Brief exploration of the Motion blocks: Right now, the blue "Motion" tab is lit up at the top of the page and you are looking at the blocks in that category.
    • Click on three or four times and watch the stage to see what the arrowhead-shaped sprite does.
    • Click on a few times to see what happens.
    • What does do?
    • What do the other blocks seem to do?
    • Change a number in one of the blocks and see how that changes what the block does.
  • Leaving a trace: The sprite moved without leaving a trace. If you want the sprite to draw—to leave a trace as it moves—it must "touch the tip of its pen" to the "paper" of the stage. The command tells it to set its penpoint down on that paper. Historical Note: Robot "Turtles" with real pens before sprites
  • Scavenger hunt—a brief exploration of other tabs: Click the other tabs to see if you can find these thirteen blocks. As you find them, click them to see if you can figure out what they do. Some have effects on the stage. Some do other things.

In this lab, we will focus on the Motion, Sound, Pen, and Sensing tabs. You will learn more about the other tabs in the next few labs.
