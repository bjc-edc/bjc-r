Working Remotely

  • Computers affect the distribution of jobs among communities by enabling long-distance work. This is why customer service operators often have Indian accents these days: They're really in India! This is good for Indian workers but not so good for American workers. Even intellectual work -- in particular, even computer programming -- can be outsourced. Cf. Amazon Mechanical Turk. (There was a news story about somebody who got hired as a programmer in the US and used Mechanical Turk to outsource his entire job, since programmers in India aren't paid as much as ones in the US. I think he got found out eventually.)
    • Sometimes computer-mediated outsourcing is a social good for reasons that aren't directly economic, such as the provision of medical care in remote rural areas by doctors in the city plus nurses in the remote areas.
  • Rural third-world farmers benefit from the ability to get up-to-date food prices in the big city, so that the wholesalers who bring their produce to the city don't take advantage of them in price negotiation.
