In this exercise, you will learn to make the Koch snowflake:

The Koch snowflake consists of three copies of the Koch curve, arranged in a triangular shape:

Unlike tree and triangle fractal, this one does not return the sprite to the same place after each recursive call. To create the Koch curve, the sprite starts on one side and ends on the other.

For example, the base case has the sprite start on the left and go to the right:

At higher levels, the algorithm repeats the next lower level four times in the pattern shown below. Look at the images below to see how the previous level is repeated in each higher level.

The colors are added here to make the visual more clear; your version can be all one color.



Each recursive call to each next lowest level makes a picture that is one third the size of the previous picture.

  1. Fill in the blanks in this recursive procedure to draw one side of the Koch snowflake.
  2. When you get one side of the snowflake working, combine three of these to make the entire snowflake.
  3. Why does the size in each recursive call have to be one third the size of the previous level? There are four copies; why not one fourth the size?
