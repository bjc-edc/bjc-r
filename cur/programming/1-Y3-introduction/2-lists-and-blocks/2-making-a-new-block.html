Improving Gossip by Making a New Block

Making a New Block

gossip2 always starts the same way. "Oh, but..." can get boring.

In this activity, you will create your own new block that varies how gossip2 starts. Your new block will work just like who and does what but you will give it several starter phrases like "Oh, but" and "I heard that" and "Who cares! Did you hear?" (or whatever you like). You will use your new block instead of the fixed text "Oh, but...."

Making new blocks is a lot like editing existing blocks.

Here's how:
  1. Open your project, U1L1-Gossip. (Make sure you are logged in.)
You can improve this program by editing who and does what and giving them more (or different) language to use!
  1. TO MAKE A NEW BLOCK,
    • Control-click (or right-click) anywhere on the scripting area except on a block, and choose "make a block..."
    • Type gossip starter as the title for this block.
    • Select the oval Reporter shape..
      (You will learn more about this selection later.)
    • From the orange palette, drag in and .
    • Click on the "1" in item, and select random.
    • Fill list with whatever you need.
    • Click OK when you're satisfied.
    2. "U1L2-Gossip"
Every block needs a place to "live" (a palette, shown by the color), a title, a category (shown by the shape of the block), and a script that defines its behavior.
  • You can put the new block in any palette that makes sense to you. If you don't choose a palette/color, your block will be a gray "Other" block and will be sorted into the "Variables" tab.
    Palette: Choose a palette for your new block to go into. ("Motion" or "Pen" might be appropriate for the draw square block.)
    • You can find more information in the Snap Manual pages 17-18.
  • Title: Give your block a name. For this block, type "draw square" as shown here.
  • Category: Leave "Command" selected since this block performs an action (drawing a square) rather than reporting a value.
  • For all sprites. In this course, you will almost always want to keep "for all sprites" selected, so you can ignore that choice for now, and click "OK".
  • Behavior: A dialog titled "Block Editor" will appear. Build the instructions for your new block here just as you do in the scripting area. Drag blocks from the palette to define the script that will run when your custom block is used. When you are finished, click OK or Apply.
    • The editor starts you with a hat block that contains a picture of your block. Remember that hat blocks say when the script below them should run: when flag clicked, when space key pressed, etc. This hat block says, "when the draw square block is used." (Don't worry about the + signs for the moment; they just let you add more words or inputs.)
