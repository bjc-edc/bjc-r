Improving Gossip by Making a New Block

Making a New Block

gossip2 always starts with "Oh, but...." That can get boring.

In this activity, you will create a block that randomly chooses starter phrases like "Oh, but" or "And did you hear that" or "Who cares!" (or whatever you like).

  1. Open your project, U1L1-Gossip. (Make sure you are logged in.)
  1. TO MAKE A NEW BLOCK,
    • Control-click (or right-click) anywhere on the scripting area except on a block, and choose "make a block..."
    • Type a title for your block.
            For this block, use the title gossip starter.
    • Select a shape..
            For this block, you need the oval Reporter shape. (You'll learn why later.)
    • Drag in the blocks you need and build the script.
            For this block, you need and .
    • Fill any inputs that need to be filled.
            For this block, click on the "1" in item, and select random.
            Then fill list with whatever you need.
    • Click OK when you're satisfied.
  2. Now edit gossip2 and slide your new block in to replace the fixed text "Oh, but...."
  3. Check it out! Click gossip2 a few times to make sure it's working the way you want. Fix any things that still need work.
  4. Now switch back to the gray sprite and click on its script to check the whole program.
    5. Your new block will work just like who and does what.
    "U1L2-Gossip"
Every block needs a title, a category (shown by the block's shape), and a script that defines its behavior. It also needs a place to "live" (a palette, shown by the color). You can put the new block in any palette that makes sense to you. If you don't choose a palette/color, your block will be a gray "Other" block and will be sorted into the "Variables" tab.
