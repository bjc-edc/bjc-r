Linear Time

In a previous lesson, you saw that the data-plots for some reporters looked close to a non-horizontal line. One of these is list between—it reports the list of integers between its two inputs.

  1. Make a list of the average time for list between using inputs from 200 to 10000, using a sacle factor of, say, 1.5.

  2. Make a data-plot of your list. Are your points close to a line?
  3. Look carefully at the data in your list. The inputs increase by a constant scale factor. If you used 1.5, they look something like this
    200, 300, 450, 675, 1012.5, 1518.75, 2278.125, 3417.1875, 5125.78125, 7688.671875
    How do the outputs (the average times) change?
    What is an approximate equation for your data-plot?
    Look for something close to regularity in the data.
  4. Try a different scale factor—if you chose 1.5, try 2 or 1.1. Perform the same experiment. Do the average times change in any regular (or close to regular) way?
