Simulation Project: Disease Spread (Teacher's Choice)


In this project you will simulate the spread of a disease in a population.

  1. Play with the Snap! file linked here to get a feel for the simulation program you will be writing.
  2. Here are some recommended steps to help you in your coding:
    • Ask the user for the percentage of the population initially infected.
    • Use the create a clone of myself block of Snap! to generate a population of 25 agents. (More than 25 will probably slow down the simulation)
    • Make the input percentage of the population sick, make the rest healthy.
    • Start a timer to measure the duration of the simulation.
    • Have the agents move around with constant speed, bounce at the edges.
    • When a healthy agent collides with a sick agent it also becomes sick.
    • Stop the animations and the timer when the entire population becomes sick.
    3. Try your very best but if you get stuck, you can use this page for hints.
  1. Study the relation between P, the percentage of the population initially sick and T, time elapsed until the entire population becomes sick. Make chart of P versus T, in increments of 5% for P. Divide labor in class as needed to build the chart. To ensure reliability you may need to run each simulation several times and take the average. Create a graph of your findings. Do you see any patterns?
  2. Here are some variations on your simulation that make it more realistic. Save a copy of your work and then implement one or more of these to see how your simulation changes:
    • Introduce vaccination at the start of simulation to a given percentage of the population.
    • Give a probability to being infected when a healthy agent encounters a sick agent.
    • Let the infected heal either with time or by a probability.
    • Let the infected slow down in speed.
    • Let those infected and then healed become immune to sickness.
    • Introduce doctor agents that heal the sick upon encounter.
  3. Design and implement a Predator-Prey simulation. What are some realistic factors you need to incorporate into your model so you can observe the dynamic fluctuations in the populations of predator and prey?
