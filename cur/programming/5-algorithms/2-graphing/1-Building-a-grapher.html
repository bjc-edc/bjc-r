But what if you wanted to plot the popularity of a baby name over time, like this?

The y-values 0, 0.1, 0.2, 0.3, etc., are so close to zero that they would all look the same. The huge increase in popularity—the name 8 times as popular in 1980 as in 1960—would not be visible at all. In fact, none of the graph would be visible because the x-values would all be off the screen.

A grapher needs to be able to specify the scale of the screen.
