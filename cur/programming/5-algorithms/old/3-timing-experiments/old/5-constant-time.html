Constant Time

One of the simplest reporters reporters has to be "add 1''.

    Pick some small inputs and some very big ones.

You probably noticed that the computer takes approximately the same time to add 1 a number, no matter how large the number is. This is why computer scientists call adding 1 to number a constant-time operation. It turns out that any basic arithmetic operation (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, and exponentiation) is a constant-time operation.

Try timing the "multiply by 3" reporter. What about "add 275?".

Constant-time operations are prized by computer scienctists, but, unfortunately, most algorithms are not constant-time.
