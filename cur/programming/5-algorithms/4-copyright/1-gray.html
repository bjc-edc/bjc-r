Who is Elisha Gray? (No fair Googling!)

He invented the phonograph.
No, that invention is generally credited to Thomas Edison in 1877, but see also Charles Cros, who independently invented it, also in 1877.
He was a 19th Century artist.
No, he did live in the 19th Century, but he was an engineer, not an artist.
He wrote Oliver Twist.
No, that was Charles Dickens, in 1837-39.
He invented the telephone.
Yes! Wait, wasn't that Alexander Graham Bell?

Who is Antonio Meucci? (No fair Googling!)

He invented the steam engine.
No, that invention is generally credited to James Watt in 1781, but the first steam engine was actually patented by Jerónimo de Ayanz y Beaumont 175 years earlier, in 1606.
He was a 19th Century artist.
No, he did live in the 19th Century, but he was an engineer, not an artist.
He wrote Il Principe.
No, that was Niccolò Machiavelli, in 1513.
He invented the telephone.
Yes! In 1871, five years before Bell and Gray. He filed a "patent caveat" (notice of intention to file a patent) that year, but didn't file the full patent application.
