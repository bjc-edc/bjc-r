On this page, you'll use abstraction to create a "Sentence Builder" project that builds complicated sentences like "the little elephant runs happily around the wise pizza" by joining different parts of speech: nouns, verbs, adjectives, articles, possessive pronouns, adverbs, prepositions, etc.

The examples will be in English. For this kind of exercise, English is less complicated than some other languages, but you may use any language you like.

Create a more flexible sentence builder. Some ideas to try:

  1. Occasionally include people's names instead of a noun phrase. So, instead of something like "my silly elephant", the program could sometimes use "Alfred" or some other names.
  2. Noun phrases don't always have to have one adjective. They can have none or two, etc.
    3. Your old Snap! project should be called "U2Lab1-Plural".
  3. Use your plural block from Unit 2 to let your nouns sometimes be plural. Your program will need two separate expressions, one for singular sentences and one for plural ones, since the verb has to match the noun: "the kangaroo jumps" but "the kangaroos jump."
    4. Also, in part D, we should think about whether this is the simplest kind of question. Another candidate would be "Who ...?" which just requires changing noun phrase.
  4. Find a way to create questions. (The sentence could start with a question phrase like "Why does", "Did", "How did", etc.)
    5. You might want to create a question verb phrase block that is a version of verb phrase that reports the question form of the verb (without the "s" as the end).
