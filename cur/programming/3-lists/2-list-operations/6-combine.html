These operations are associative, which means that the result is the same no matter how the items are grouped. For example, (3 + 4) + 5 = 3 + (4 + 5) and join words("the cat")("sat") = join words("the")("cat sat").
You are allowed to drag other blocks like or , and you'll get an answer, but it may not be what you expect. In general, don't use subtraction and division because the grouping of the items matters in these cases. For example, (3 - 4) - 5 ≠ 3 - (4 - 5). The expression actually gives the result to 3 - (4 - 5).
