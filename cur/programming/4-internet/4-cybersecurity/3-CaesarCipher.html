Caesar Cipher Project (Teacher's Choice)


A Caesar cipher (or shift cipher) is a simple and widely known encryption method. It's a substitution cipher. Each letter in the plaintext (un-encrypted text) shifts some fixed number of positions down the alphabet. For example, “ABC123abc” shifted by 4 becomes “EFG567efg”. This technique is named after Gaius Julius Caesar, an influential Roman who ruled 49-44 BC and used encryption in his correspondence.

In this project you will develop a simple message encryption program, using a method based on the “Caesar Cipher”.
