Selection Sort

One of the most commonly used sorts is called a selection sort. Here's how it works:

  • The earliest item might be the first name in alphabetical order, or the smallest number in a list.
    Step 1. In an unsorted list, select the item that should come earliest in the sort.
  • Step 2. Pull the item out of the list and place it first.
  • Step 3. With the remaining items, select the item that should come next in the sort.
  • Step 4. Pull the item out of the list and place it.
  • Step 5. Repeat Steps 3 and 4 until there is only one item left.
