Getting Started

You're in a class with a total of five students: Rocky, Bullwinkle, Peabody, Sherman, and (your name here). You want to choose a committee to get the refreshments for the class party. Remember that "Rocky and Sherman" is the same committee as "Sherman and Rocky."

  1. How many different committees of three people include Bullwinkle?
  2. Fill in the blanks: "The number of committees of three people that include Bullwinkle is equal to the number of committees of ______ people that don't ______________."
  3. How many different committees of three people don't include Bullwinkle?
  4. Fill in the blanks: "The number of committees of three people that don't include Bullwinkle is equal to the number of committees of three people chosen from _____________."
  5. How many different committees of three are there altogether?
  6. How many different committees of two?
  7. How many different committees of one? (Is this a sensible questions?)
  8. How many different committees of zero people? (This is for sure not a sensible question, but it still has a sensible answer. Discuss it with other students if you can't find one.)
  9. How many different committees of five people are there?
  1. Write a committees block that takes a number of people and a list of names as input, and reports a list of all the possible committees (a list of lists, where each smaller list contains the names in a committee).
