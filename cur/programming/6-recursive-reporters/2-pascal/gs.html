Getting Started

 

 

You're in a class with a total of five students: Rocky, Bullwinkle, Peabody, Sherman, and (your name here). You want to choose a committee to get the refreshments for the class party. Remember that "Rocky and Sherman" is the same committee as "Sherman and Rocky."

  1. How many different committees of three people include Bullwinkle?
  2. If a committee of three people includes Bullwinkle, how many more people are on the committee?
  3. If a committee of three people includes Bullwinkle, how many other students (besides Bullwinkle) are eligible to be on the committee?
  4. How many committees of two people could you make from a class of four students?
  5. How many different committees of three people don't include Bullwinkle?
  6. If a committee doesn't include Bullwinkle, how many students are eligible to be on it?
  7. How many committees of three people could you make from a class of four students?
  8. How many different committees of three people are there altogether in a group of five students?
  9. How many 2-person committees can you make from 5 students??
  10. How many 3-person committees can you make from 6 students? See if you can use the results from earlier questions to help answer this one.
  11. How many 1-member committees can you form from 100 students?
  12. How many 99-member committees can you form from 100 students?
  13. How many 100-member committees can you form from 100 students?
  14. How many 0-member committees can you make from 100 students? (Does it make any sense to talk about a 0-member committee?)
  15. How did you solve question 10? Describe your method in words, so precisely that others in your class understand it.
