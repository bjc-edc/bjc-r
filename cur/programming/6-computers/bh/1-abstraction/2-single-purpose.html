The abacus was first invented about 4,000 years ago. It is a computing device that can be used to do arithmetic, even including square roots and cube roots, on multi-digit numbers. In some countries, abaci are still widely used today. People who are well-trained to use the abacus can perform calculations remarkably quickly.

example of a device for special-purpose "computation"; edit way back
There have been many inventions, over the course of history, of special-purpose computing devices. A typical example is the orrery, a device made to display the positions of the planets in the Solar System. The first known device of this kind was the Antikythera mechanism from about 2100 years ago. It was discovered in 1900, in fragments, in an ancient shipwreck in Greece. At the time it was made, it was generally believed that the sun and the other planets all revolved around the Earth. This made the positions very complicated to work out, so the mechanism was much harder to design and build than it would be today.
