Logic Gates: AND, OR, NOT

Computers deal with 0' and 1's and you may wonder how they are able to execute their vast variety of complex tasks -involving thousands of decisions and computations- using only such simple binary digits. At the heart of digital electronics are elementary devices called logic gates which emulate Boolean functions you are already familiar with, such as : AND, OR and NOT. The inputs to these functions are binary (False/True or 0/1) as is the output. In this lab you will catch a glimpse of how electronic circuitry can mimic Boolean operators and how elementary logic gates can be combined to manipulate and process binary digits to perform complex arithmetic tasks such as adding two numbers.
