Reporters

At the bottom of Motion palette are three blocks shaped differently from the others. These oval-shaped blocks are called reporters. Unlike the jigsaw-puzzle-piece-shaped command blocks we've used until now, reporters don't carry out an action (such as moving the sprite or displaying a speech balloon) by themselves. Instead, they report a value, usually for use in another block's input slot.
The and reporters tell you where the sprite is on the stage. As in algebra, x means left-to-right position, and y means bottom-to-top position.
  • Drag an x position block into the scripting area and click on it. You should see a little speech balloon next to the block:
  • Now, click on the sprite itself, drag it somewhere else and click on the x position block again.
  • Click on the gray box to the left of the x position block in the palette, and then look over to the stage. You should see that the value that the block would report is displayed on the stage: This on-stage display is called a watcher.
  • Create a watcher for y position and also for direction. Move the sprite around, using commands or just by dragging, and see how the values reported by these blocks change.
