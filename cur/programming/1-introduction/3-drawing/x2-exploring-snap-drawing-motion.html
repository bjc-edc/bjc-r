Take It Further!

  1. At the right is a painting by Alma Thomas. It looks like a stylized version of a map.
    It's your art. Take liberties.

    I tried to think of a way to begin by laying out streets and then positioning brown houses beside them. But I couldn't figure out how. Then I thought, Thomas's picture is more brown than white. What if I start with a solid brown background and then lay down white roads?

    The way to get a solid color background is to tell the stage to use that color:
    (It's a little strange because the stage doesn't have a pen, but it interprets that command to mean that it should color the entire background that color.)

    Then it's just a matter of plotting some meandering white roads, by turning a smallish random number of degrees after each move.

    Thomas's original map has what looks like the center of town, with the town hall and so on, at the right edge of the picture. Having failed at a faithful reproduction of the obvious map-ness of her work, I wanted to get some of the details similar, so when one of my meandering roads started near the right end of the stage, I used a thicker white pen to paint it.

    But don't follow my plan any more rigidly than you would hers. Do whatever appeals to you.
