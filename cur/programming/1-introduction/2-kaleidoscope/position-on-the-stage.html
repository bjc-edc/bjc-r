Position on the Stage

You already know how to determine the (x, y) coordinates of the sprite. You can, if you like, also change the appearance of the stage to show the axes (or other backgrounds). The picture below shows the stage decorated by axes and a grid. It also gives the coordinates of the four points that are on the axes and farthest from the origin but still visible on the stage.
To make the snap stage look like this:
  1. In the Sprite Corral, select the Stage.
  2. Then, choose "Backgrounds" from the File menu .
  3. And choose "xy-grid.gif".
Coordinate systems for computer screens do not all work the way we expect in algebra, but snap's does. In the picture above, the tip of the sprite's arrowhead is to the right of the origin, so its x position is positive. Since it is also below the origin, its y position is negative.
Unlike the common mathematical convention, the numbers naming direction start with 0 at the top ("north") and count degrees going clockwise. The black sprite above is pointing to the "east," so its direction is 90.
