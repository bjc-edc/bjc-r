As the first class and the first lab, it is important that students know what is expected of them. Labs are intended to be times during which students work at their own pace (in pairs). They should know that they are responsible for getting through the material in the section, but that they are also free to explore and create if something interests them. Occasionally, there are prompts in the labs directing students to post their work, whether it be to a Google classroom feed, Piazza, or some other platform. Allow time for the students (and yourself) to get used to the technology. Expect glitches, and also model the necessary patience and perseverance necessary to work with new technology.

Students will:

  • Practice pair programming
  • Create a snap account
  • Explore the snap interface by searching for blocks
  • Create their first scripts to draw a square (and a triangle or hexagon)
  • Use reporter blocks to explore x- and y-positions on the screen

To prepare:

  • Make your own snap account using your classroom's technology
  • Think about and set up other technology (Google classroom, Piazza, etc.) you'd like students to use
  • Read this article about pair programming. Students will also read this later.
  • Check out these tips from csteachingtips.org:
    • Have students sign a "Computer Use Agreement" before giving them access to machines to hold them accountable for using computers responsibly.
    • Encourage students to talk to each other to decrease the burden on the teacher and to emulate industry work environments.
