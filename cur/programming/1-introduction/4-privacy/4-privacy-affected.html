Innovations and Privacy

On this page, you will discuss how computers impact privacy and read and teach each other about data-collection technologies.

  1. Discuss: What are different ways our privacy is affected by computers?
  2. Each pick two sections to read:
    • Smile While We Snap!
    • Knowing Where You Are
    • Knowing Even Where Your Shoes Are
    • Black Boxes Are Not Just for Airplanes Anymore
    • Tracing Paper
    • The Parking Garage Knows More Than You Think
    • All in Your Pocket
    • Connecting the Dots

    In groups of four, split up the reading in Blown to Bits pages 22-35. Each person should pick two sections to read from the list at right. (Make sure someone reads each section).

    As you read, use these guiding questions:
    1. What are the innovations described in each section?
    2. How are these innovations beneficial?
    3. How do they threaten privacy?
  3. Teach the other members of your group what you learned in the sections you read.
  4. Read the box How Sites Know Who You Are (Blown to Bits page 40).
  1. Read the section "Little Brother is Watching" from Blown to Bits pages 42-48 for examples of ways people can find out very personal information using online resources.
  2. What rules should we have about online information? Read the section called Fair Information Practice Principles (Blown to Bits page 64-68) to learn about efforts to enact privacy laws and standards. Consider: What laws should we have about privacy? What are some challenges to getting these laws?
