Data is ubiquitous; we work with it all the time. Throughout your life, data about you is collected, stored, copied, shared, edited, and sometimes deleted. We measure data using bits and bytes. Data size impacts storage space and transmission times.

  • 1 bit = a zero or a one
  • 1 byte = 8 bits
  • 1 kilobyte (KB or KiB) = 1024 bytes (one paragraph of text)
  • 1 megabyte (MB or MiB) = 1024 KiB (a four megapixel JPEG-compressed image)
  • 1 gigabyte (GB or GiB) = 1024 MiB (one hour of SD TV or seven minutes of HDTV)
  • 1 terabyte (TB or TiB) = 1024 GiB (2,000 hours of uncompressed audio or 17,000 hours of MP3s)
  • 1 petabyte (PB or PiB) = 1024 TiB (enough space to store the DNA of the entire population of the US three times)

Netflix is said to use 1 PB to store the videos for streaming.

World of Warcraft is stored on 1.3PB to maintain the game.

Internet Archive: About 10PB

AT&T transfers about 30PB of data through its networks each day.

YouTube processes about 40PB of videos a day.

Multimedia data is the biggest data!
