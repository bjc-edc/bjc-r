Reporters

The three oval-shaped blocks at the bottom of the Motion palette are called reporters. Unlike the jigsaw-puzzle-shaped command blocks, reporters don't carry out an action (such as moving the sprite or displaying a speech balloon). Instead, they report a value, usually for use in another block's input slot.
The and reporters tell you where the sprite is on the stage. As in algebra, x means left-to-right position, and y means bottom-to-top position.
  1. Drag an x position block into the scripting area and click on it. You should see a little speech balloon next to the block:
  2. Now, click on the sprite itself, drag it somewhere else and click on the x position block again.
  3. Click on the gray box to the left of the x position block in the palette, and then look over to the stage. The value that the block would report is displayed on the stage: This on-stage display is called a watcher.
  4. Create a watcher for y position and also for direction. Move the sprite around, using commands or just by dragging, and see how the values reported by these blocks change. Also notice what values don't change.
