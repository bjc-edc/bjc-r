Start Your First Mobile App

Program the yellow Alonzo sprite to turn around when it is clicked.

  1. Use the URL http://snap.berkeley.edu/run to launch Snap! in a new window (or tab).
    Bookmark or write down the snap link. You will need it every day.
  2. Create two costumes for the sprite, one looking in each direction:
    1. Select the sprite, , in Sprite Corral in the lower right, and click the Costumes tab above the Scripting Area in the center.
    2. Then select "Costumes..." from the File menu, and choose "alonzo.gif" to make the yellow Alonzo sprite appear in the Staging Area.
    3. To make the second sprite, right-click (or control-click on a Mac) the yellow Alonzo sprite and select "duplicate".
    4. Next, right-click (or control-click on a Mac) the second yellow Alonzo sprite and select "edit" to open the "Paint Editor."
    5. In the Paint Editor, click the "flip ⟷" button and then click "OK."

  3. Build this script. When you have built the script, click on it to see what it does.

    The animation below shows where to find the blocks and how to snap them together.

    Drag one block underneath another to snap them together. The white bar shows where the block will snap.
    The blocks' colors match their palettes: purple for looks, yellow for Control, etc.

  4. To start the program, click the green flag in the upper right corner of the window. If your script works, then, when you click on Alonzo it should face the other way.
