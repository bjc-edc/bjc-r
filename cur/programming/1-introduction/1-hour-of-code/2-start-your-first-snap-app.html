Start Your First Snap! App

Program the gray arrow-head sprite to look like Alonzo and to turn around when it is clicked.

  1. Bookmark or write down the snap link. You will need it every day.
    Use the URL http://snap.berkeley.edu/run to launch Snap! in a new window (or tab).
  2. Create two costumes for the sprite (one looking in each direction). You can follow these instructions or watch the animation below.
    1. Select "Costumes..." from the File menu, . Choose "alonzo.gif". The Alonzo costume will appear.
    2. To make the second costume, right-click (or control-click on a Mac) the Alonzo costume in the costume area and select "duplicate."
    3. Then right-click (control-click) the new costume, select "edit" to open the "Paint Editor," and click the "flip ⟷" button. Then click "OK."

  3. Drag one block underneath another to snap them together. The white bar shows where the block will snap.

    The blocks' colors match their palettes: purple for Looks, yellow for Control, etc.
    Click the "Scripts" tab above the staging area, and drag blocks into it to build this script.

    The animation shows where to find the blocks and how to snap them together.
  4. Click on Alonzo. If your script works, Alonzo should face the other way.
