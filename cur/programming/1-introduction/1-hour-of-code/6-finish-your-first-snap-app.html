Finish Your First Snap! App

In Snap!, a sprite can have more than one script controlling its behavior. You currently have just one script, which runs when you click on Alonzo. Now you'll make a second script that runs as soon as the green flag is clicked to welcome the player to your game with a message from Alonzo.

Clicking the green flag, , is one way to start a Snap! script. You can stop all running scripts by clicking the red stop sign, .
    Hint: You can do this using just 3 blocks!
  1. Create a second script that makes Alonzo move to (190, -130) and say a welcome message (like, "Welcome to my Hour of Code project! Keep clicking me if you can!") whenever the green flag at the top right, , is clicked.
  2. Make that message disappear when the player clicks on Alonzo.
    Using the Say block with an empty input will get Alonzo to stop saying whatever he's saying.

  3. Check that your app does everything you intend it to do:
    • When the green flag is clicked, Alonzo should move to (190, -130) and say a welcome message.
    • When Alonzo is clicked, the message should disappear, and Alonzo should move to a random spot on the stage and face the other way.
