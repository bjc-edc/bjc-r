Polygonal Racetracks

In this project, you are going to explore the turning angles needed to trace or draw regular polygons.
A regular polygon is a geometric figure in which all the sides and angles are equal.

Imagine that you have a racecar that needs to travel on a racetrack in the shape of a regular polygon. Every time the car reaches a corner, it needs to make a turn in order to stay on track. But, how much is this turn? And exactly how does it depend on the number of sides of the polygon?

  1. Use this interactive program to see demonstrations of how the racecar would travel in such a track.
Notice that (unlike what intuition might tell) the turning is done not by the interior but by the exterior angle of the polygon.

An easy way to do this is to notice that if you start from a corner and keep traveling along the polygon making a turn at each corner until you come back to where you started, you have made a full cycle of 360o. A bird’s-eye view of the tracks shown on the right might make this clearer.
