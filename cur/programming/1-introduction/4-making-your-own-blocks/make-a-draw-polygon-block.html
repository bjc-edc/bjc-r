Make a Draw Polygon Block

This is the kind of project where pair programming can be especially useful.

  1. Now, create a draw polygon block that takes two inputs:
    • a number of sides and
    • the length of each side.
    You learned how to draw a regular polygon in a previous lesson. How many degrees altogether? How many turns?

    In the block, call these inputs sides and length.

    Be sure to test out your solution with several polygons, with different lengths and number of sides.

  1. Look back, if you like, to see how you nested squares in squares. Figure out how to create a new procedure nested polygons with two or three levels of nesting, and inputs that tell how many sides and what side length to use.
