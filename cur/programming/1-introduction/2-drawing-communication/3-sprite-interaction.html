    You may want each "tiny amount" to be even less than 1 step so that Sprite(2) doesn't catch Sprite too quickly.
    This kind of written-out code is often called "pseudo-code." It isn't precisely what your code would look like in any programming language but it makes the logical steps clear. There are no special "rules" for writing pseudo-code. You may use your own style as long as you specify the logical steps clearly.

You can make this more interesting in lots of ways, if you like.

  1. You can give the sprites costumes. You can even use a photograph or picture from the Internet by dragging the picture into the costume area for that sprite. If the picture you choose is too big, set its size to something less than 100%.
  2. You can make a dialogue about school or home or work or play.
  3. You can add a third sprite and have it do something, too.
  4. You can even change the background on the stage.
