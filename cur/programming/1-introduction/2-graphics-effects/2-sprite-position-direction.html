Useful notes:
  • Once the pen is down, it stays down even in a different script. If you want your script to move the sprite without leaving a trace, use the pen up block to "lift the pen."
  • If you want, you can use watchers to show the direction of the sprite or its x and y coordinates.
  • Coordinate systems for computer screens do not all work the way we expect in algebra, but snap’ does. Unlike the common mathematical convention, the numbers naming direction start with 0 at the top ("north") and count degrees going clockwise. The black sprite above is pointing to the "east," so its direction is 90.

Watchers 

The three oval-shaped reporters at the bottom of the Motion palette have checkboxes for watchers. Unlike the jigsaw-puzzle-shaped command blocks, reporters don't carry out an action (such as moving the sprite or displaying a speech balloon). Instead, they report a value, usually for use in another block's input slot.

If you click the reporters, they will tell you their value (in this case, who the sprite is positioned on the stage). If you check off the boxes next to them, their values will be displayed in a watcher on the stage. As in algebra, x means left-to-right position, and y means bottom-to-top position.

There are reporters with checkboxes for watchers in several of the snap palettes.
