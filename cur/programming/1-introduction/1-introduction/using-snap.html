snap is a programming language. Programs are sets of instructions (called scripts) that tell the computer what to do.

Since snap is a visual language, you will build your programs by dragging blocks (individual instructions) and snapping them into place.

Create a snap Account

Creating an account will allow you to save your programs.

  1. Open snap using this URL: http://snap.berkeley.edu/run, and bookmark it so you can access it easily.
  2. Click the cloud menu in the toolbar:
  3. Select the "sign up" option in the menu, and follow the instructions.
  4. Check your email to get your initial password.
    5. You may change your password at any time, using the cloud menu, .
    Always choose passwords that you can remember but are hard for others to guess.
  5. Return to snap, and use your username together with the initial password to log in.
  6. Select "Change Password..." from the cloud menu, and change your password to something you will remember.

Pair Programming Swap

In this course, you will use a technique that professional programmers call pair programming—writing computer programs in teams of two people. One partner writes the program; the other partner helps plan and helps catch errors.

Switch roles often, aiming for equal time in each role. If you haven't swapped yet, swap now.
