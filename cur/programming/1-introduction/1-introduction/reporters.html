Reporters

The three oval-shaped blocks at the bottom of the Motion palette are called reporters. Unlike the jigsaw-puzzle-shaped command blocks, reporters don't carry out an action (such as moving the sprite or displaying a speech balloon). Instead, they report a value, usually for use in another block's input slot.

The and reporters tell you where the sprite is on the stage. As in algebra, x means left-to-right position, and y means bottom-to-top position.

  1. Drag an x position block into the scripting area and click on it. You should see a speech balloon next to the block:
  2. Now, click on the sprite on the stage, drag it somewhere and click on the x position block again.
  3. You can create a watcher that sits on the stage and constantly displays the x-position of the sprite. In the palette, click on the gray box to the left of the x position block. Now drag the sprite around and see what that watcher says.
  4. Create watchers for y position and direction. Move the sprite around, using commands or just by dragging it with your mouse. Notice which values change and which values don't.
