Your stage should look like this with axes, a grid, the origin, and 3 watchers: x position, y position, and direction.

In some programming languages, the origin is at the upper left corner of the screen, and the numbers get bigger as the sprite moves down the screen. They use that system because TV screens display from top to bottom. The use of more familiar center-origin coordinates in snap is an example of abstraction: hiding implementation details so that the user can think in more familiar ways.

The coordinate system is a little different from the one you may have learned in algebra.
  • In snap, positions are the same as in algebra: the origin, (0, 0), is in the middle of the stage, horizontal position is called x and is positive to the right of the origin, and vertical position is called y and is positive above the origin.
  • Unlike mathematical convention, the sprite direction in snap starts with 0 at the top and counts up in degrees going clockwise. This is the numbering used for compass directions.
