Programming Your App

You and a partner will program together on one computer. This game is like Whack-a-mole. You try to click on Alonzo, but Alonzo plays hard to catch by moving randomly around the stage.

  1. Make sure you have Snap! open http://snap.berkeley.edu/run and that you are logged in.
  2. Create two "Alonzo" costumes for your sprite. The video below shows how.
The instructions summarize the video.
  • Select "Costumes..." from the menu. Choose Alonzo.
  • Right-click (on a Mac, control-click) the Alonzo costume in the gray costume area (not on the white stage) and select "duplicate."
  • Right-click (control-click) the new costume, select "edit."
  • Click the "flip ⟷" button. Then, click "OK."
  1. To program the sprite to change costumes when it is clicked:
    1. click the "Scripts" tab above the gray costume area,
    2. drag blocks into it to build this script:

You can find blocks in palettes with matching color: purple for Looks, yellow for Control, etc.

Drag one block underneath another to snap them together. The white bar shows where the blocks will snap together if you unclick the mouse.

This animation shows where to find the blocks and how to snap them together:

  1. Click on Alonzo on the white stage. If your script works, Alonzo should face the other way.

Saving your work and continuing to program

It is good practice to save regularly so that you don't lose the work that you've done.
(If you have not already created an account, do that now.)
  1. Click to see if you are logged in.
    1. IF you see this menu: , just log in with your username.
    2. IF you see this menu: , check that the username you see is yours.
      • IF NOT your username, choose "Logout." Then click the cloud again and log in with your username.
      • IF it is your username, choose "Save" from the menu, and give your project a name that will help you remember what the project is.
        For this project, use U1L1-ClickAlonzo.
        "U1L1-ClickAlonzo"

    2. Only one partner needs to save now. You'll share the project with both partners at the end of this lab.
  2. As always, click NEXT (top of page) to continue working, or log out If you're done.
