Starting Your First Snap! App

You and a partner will work together on one computer to program a game you can run on your phones. The point of the game is for you to click on Alonzo as much as you can. Alonzo makes it harder by moving randomly around the screen.

It doesn't matter which of you is logged in. You'll learn how to share the project with the other partner in a few pages.

This is Alonzo.
  1. Run Snap! in a separate window (or tab).
  2. Create two "Alonzo" costumes for the sprite. You can follow these instructions or watch the animation below:
    1. Go to the menu and select "Costumes...". Choose "Alonzo". (If you don't see "Costumes..." click the sprite thumbnail image in the lower right and try again.)
    2. Right-click (on a Mac, control-click) the Alonzo costume in the gray costume area (not on the white stage) and select "duplicate."
    3. Then, right-click (control-click) the new costume, select "edit," and click the "flip ⟷" button.
      Then, click "OK."


  3. Find blocks in palettes with matching color: purple for Looks, yellow for Control, etc.
    To program the sprite to change costumes when it is clicked:
    1. click the "Scripts" tab above the gray costume area,
    2. drag blocks into it to build this script:

    Drag one block underneath another to snap them together. The white bar shows where the blocks will snap together if you unclick the mouse.

    This animation shows where to find the blocks and how to snap them together:

  4. Click on Alonzo on the white stage. If your script works, Alonzo should face the other way.
