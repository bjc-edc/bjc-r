This is Alonzo, the mascot of our programming language, Snap!. You should be seeing him on the stage, the white area in the top right corner of the Snap! window.

  1. You're going to write a game program in which the object of the game is to click on Alonzo as he's moving around. As a first step, make Alonzo jump someplace else on the stage when he's clicked. Drag these two blocks into the center area of the window:

    You can find blocks in palettes with their matching color: gold for Control, blue for Motion, etc. Drag one block underneath another to snap them together. A bunch of blocks clicked together are called a script.

    This is a computer program. It's a very short one, but it'll get more interesting soon.

  2. Check your program: Click on the Alonzo sprite on the white stage in the upper right area of the window. If your script works, Alonzo should move to a random position on the stage each time you click him.
