Using the Random Block

On this page, you'll add code to make Alonzo move to a random position each time he's clicked.
The blocks you'll need can be found in the Motion and Operators palettes.
  1. If you don't already have your U1L1-ClickAlonzo project open, open it now.
    2. Click and select "Open..." to see a list of all your projects.
  2. Find in the green Operators palette, and click it several times to see what it does.
  3. Describe clearly what the random block does.
  4. Use the go to and random blocks together to made Alonzo move around randomly.
    1. Snap the block onto the end of your script.
    2. Snap a block into each input slot of . The animation shows how, but you'll need to do more than it shows.
    3. Click on Alonzo. If you've left the 1 and 10 in the input slots of Alonzo will move only tiny distances.
    4. Change the inputs to so Alonzo moves to random coordinates, with x varying from -190 and 190, and y varying from -130 through 130.
    5. Click Alonzo. If your script works, Alonzo should face the other way and move to a random spot.
    5. Locations on the stage (the white rectangle where sprites perform their scripts) are specified by coordinates. The center of the stage is (0, 0). The left edge is x = -240; the right edge is x = 240. The bottom and top are y = -180 and y = 180.
  5. Discuss why it makes sense to limit Alonzo's movements to less than the full range of the stage (for example, by limiting his horizontal movements to between -190 to 190 instead of the full -240 to 240).
