Adding Variety to Gossip

Here is the old version of this page --MF, 4/12/19

On this page, you will improve your program so that, sometimes, the sentences are a bit longer and more complex.

The block has been built for you. It randomly chooses 1, 2, 3, or 4, and if that number is 3, then it reports something more complicated than who. Otherwise, it reports who, just as before.
  1. Open your U1L2-Gossip file.
  2. Find , drag it into the scripting area, and click on enough times to see how it's different from who.
  3. Edit this new block to see how it works. The comments may help.
  4. Here is the script inside more complicated who. What change to this script will make the more complicated phrase appear more often?
    Change the 4 to 5.
    Then three will be the randomly chosen number one out of five times instead of one out of four, and so we'll have a complicated subject less often.
    Change the 4 to 3.
    Correct! Three will then be the chosen number one out of three times, and so we'll have a complicated subject more often.
    Change the 3 to 1.
    Three will still be the randomly chosen number one out of four times, and we'll have a complicated subject just as often as before.
    Change the 3 to 5.
    The chosen number can never be 5, so we'll never get a complicated subject.

    • These oval-shaped blocks are called reporters because they report a value.
      • AAP-2.B.2
    • A reporter with its inputs filled in is an expression. In fact, anything you can put in an input slot is an expression.
    : Commands and Statements
    • Say and broadcast are command blocks; they tell the computer to do something without reporting a value.
      • AAP-2.B.2
    • A command block with all its inputs filled in is an instruction or statement.
