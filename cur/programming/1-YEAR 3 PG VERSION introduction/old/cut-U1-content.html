Watchers 

The three oval-shaped reporters at the bottom of the Motion palette have checkboxes for watchers. Unlike the jigsaw-puzzle-shaped command blocks, reporters don't carry out an action (such as moving the sprite or displaying a speech balloon). Instead, they report a value, usually for use in another block's input slot.

If you click the reporters, they will tell you their value (in this case, where the sprite is positioned on the stage). If you check the boxes next to them, their values will be displayed in a watcher on the stage. As in algebra, x means left-to-right position, and y means bottom-to-top position. Once you put a watcher on the stage, the value it displays will be kept up to date as your program runs. (You can uncheck the box to remove a watcher from the stage.)

There are reporters with checkboxes for watchers in several of the snap palettes.
