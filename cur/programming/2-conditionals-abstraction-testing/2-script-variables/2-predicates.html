Useful Predicates

snap has a "greater than" operator (>), an "equal" operator (=), and a "less than" operator (<) built in, but doesn't have a "greater than or equal to" (>=) operator.

  1. Build >= and test it well to make sure it does what you expect. Here are three things worth knowing.
    1. When you type the title of this block in the initial Make a block dialog, make sure that you choose the hexagonal predicate block shape.

      Operators like that stand in between their two inputs are called infix operators. Operators like that stand their inputs are called prefix operators. When we write n! to mean "n factorial," the "!" is a postfix operator—it comes after its input .
      Because you want the block's name>=to go between the two input slots you'll have to use the left plus sign in the Block Editor.
