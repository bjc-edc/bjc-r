Notes about BtB Chapter 3

  • What You See Is Not What the Computer Knows (p 73-80) - Includes section on metadata.
  • Representations, Reality, and Illusion (p 80-94) - How digital photography works, bits, [note: have students play with a rasterizer], image quality, preserving and losing information, lossless and lossy compression, open source software (maybe skip the part about open source software)
  • Hiding Information in Images (p 95-99) - First part is about spam (maybe skip because outdated? It's about spam in the form of images), but second part (97-99) is about steganography, which is interesting!
  • The Scary Secrets of Old Disks (p 99-108) - First part is about erasing data from disks, disk organization itself, and then a philosophical bit about data and whether turning everything digital actually reduces its lifespan as technologies become obsolete.
