Blocks, like greater than, that report true when a condition is met and false when it is not met are called "predicates."

In snap, these functions have the hexagonal shape: . Predicate functions are like questions that can be answered with true or false. The two special values are represented in snap by the blocks and . They are called Boolean values, named after the great logician George Boole.
