      But the English word "or" has two different meanings:
      • In a sentence like "It will be either raining or sunny tomorrow," the word "or" means that it may be raining, or it may be sunny, but not both. This kind of "or" is called exclusive or.
      • In a sentence like "Circle all the pictures that are either brown or a horse," you would circle a brown cow, a white horse, or a brown horse. This kind of "or" is called an inclusive or.
