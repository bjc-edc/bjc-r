From Observation Notes Analysis:
  • U2L3p1: "In problem 1, what they’re mostly struggling with is abstraction. The only "math" (in the trad sense of facts or calculations) involved is deciding what size to make an individual cell of the n-by-n array, given n and some intended overall width. Nobody had any difficulty knowing what that calculation should be, but some did have difficulty sorting out where to use it."
  • U2L3p1: "In problems 2 and A, it’s easy to see them trying to invoke mathematical "things" they know that they know, but struggling to figure out what those ideas all mean. Many succeeded with a fully general solution—a function that took board size, n, and sprite coordinates and computed the cell number from that—and some did it in a way that had a useful substructure—independently computing the row and the column and then using those to compute the cell. But there were lots of ideas that led in unproductive directions, too. "
