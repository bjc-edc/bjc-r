Using if...else... to control a block's behavior

The block has been built for you. It randomly chooses 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, or 6. IF the number is greater than 4, it reports something more complicated than who. ELSE it reports who, just as before. This algorithm produces a more complex sentence about one third of the time.
  1. Open your U1L2-Gossip file.
  2. Find , drag it into the scripting area, and click on enough to see how it's different from who.
  3. Edit it to see how it works. The comments may help.
Here is the script inside more complicated who. What change to this script will make the more complicated phrase more often?
(a) Change the 4 to 5.
Only one number chosen randomly from 1 through 6 will be greater than 5. That will happen less often, so we'll have a complicated subject less often.
(b) Change the 6 to 4.
Then the chosen number can never be greater than 4, and we'll never get a complicated subject.
(c) Change the 6 to 8.
That does increase the chance that the chosen number will be greater than 4, and therefore that the subject will be more complicated.
(d) Change the 1 to 4.
The chosen number can then be 4, 5, or 6, so we'll get a more complicated subject two-thirds of the time instead of one-third of the time.
(e) Both (a) and (b).
Those will both make the more complicated subject happen less often. If you're not sure why, click those answers to get more feedback.
(f) Both (c) and (d).
Yes, both make the more complicated subject happen more often. If you're not sure why, click those answers to get more feedback.
