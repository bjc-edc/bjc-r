On this page, you will allow the user of your gossip program to choose the sprite costumes.

Making the Computer Change the Sprite Costume

  1. If it isn't open already, open your U2L1-ClickAlonzo project.
    2. You learned how to add costumes on Unit 1 Lab 2 Page 2: Customizing and Debugging.
  2. Add several costumes for each sprite.
  3. Use to switch the sprite's costume a few times.

When you used item of before (for example, inside who and does what) , you were accessing elements of the list randomly. But you can also select a specific element by specifying its position.

: Index

The position number is called the index of the element in the list.

Lists can contain anything: letters or words, costumes, others lists, or even blocks. You can use my (costumes) to report a list of the costumes for your sprite.

And you can use item of together with my (costumes) to select a costume in a specific position in the list.

  1. Decide which costume you want for your sprite, and use switch to costume together with item of and my (costumes) to make it the costume for your sprite.
    2. You can drag blocks into the input slot of switch to costume even though it's a drop-down menu.
  2. Then decide on a different costume, and adjust your code to apply that costume. Try it a few times.

Letting the Player Choose the Sprite Costume
