An Introduction to Computational Art with
the Beauty and Joy of Computing Curriculum

Workshop description

Beauty is compelling! One of the great ways to have students fall in love with coding is by introducing them to the field of computational art in a beginning level computer programming class. As students play with computational algorithms to create beautiful artistic forms, they will be delighted to see how easy it is to generate exciting variations of their artworks through simple alterations of their code. This process will empower them and encourage them to delve deeper into advanced applications of Computer Science. In this hands-on session, we will catch a glimpse of the possibilities that computational art can offer in your classroom by trying out example projects from the Beauty and Joy of Computing curriculum, a free online AP CSP course jointly developed by UC Berkeley and EDC. In our explorations, we will use Snap!, a powerful block-based programming language inspired by Scratch. We will also demo a spectrum of artistic styles that are algorithmic, such as Islamic art and Op art. The workshop will include a demonstration of the power of recursion in algorithmic art as we imitate and extend the works of the great Dutch artist Piet Mondrian.

Examples of Computational Art Projects in BJC

Blocks with Inputs, Looping with a Counter, Graphics and Art, Sprite Line Art Project, Daisy Design
Modeling Art, Mandala Design, Fractal Art, Egyptian Motif, Animation Studio, Op Art, Recursive Mondrian
