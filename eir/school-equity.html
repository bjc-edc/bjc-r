BJC School Equity Program

The BJC School Equity Program will provide schools with a research-based school support strategy that will help establish or expand AP Computer Science Principles (AP CSP) course offerings using the Beauty and Joy of Computing (BJC) curriculum.

Apply to the BJC School Equity Program!

Benefits

  • Free AP CSP student and teacher curriculum materials
  • A $1000 stipend for each participating teacher
  • Fifty hours of online PD during the summer and throughout the school year
  • Consultations to increase student participation and success

School Eligibility

  • Public high school in the US
  • Serving students from low-income families
  • Serving students from groups typically underrepresented in CS including girls, Black, and Hispanic or Latinx students

School and Teacher Commitment

  • Offer a BJC AP CSP course in 2023–24 and 2024–25
  • Teacher participation in BJC summer 2023 PD
  • Teacher participation in twice-yearly consultations with BJC staff
  • School and teacher completion of surveys
  • District responds to anonymized student data requests

About BJC

Beauty and Joy of Computing is a College Board-endorsed, AP CSP curriculum designed to provide students with a rigorous and engaging introduction to CS and to expand access and participation in CS for all students, with an emphasis on student groups historically underrepresented in CS. BJC uses the blocks-based programming language Snap!, which offers a low-threshold of entry yet a high-ceiling of powerful programming capacity. BJC provides a robust set of teacher supports as well as an active online teacher community.

About this Project

Education Development Center (EDC) has received funding from the U.S. Department of Education to research and develop the BJC School Equity Program centered on the implementation of the Beauty and Joy of Computing curriculum. June Mark is the Principal Investigator.
