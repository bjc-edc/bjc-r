Interested in strengthening computer science at your high school?

Beauty and Joy of Computing (BJC) is a College Board-endorsed Advanced Placement (AP) Computer Science Principles (CSP) course designed to provide students with a rigorous and engaging introduction to computer science (CS) and to expand access and participation in CS for all students, with an emphasis on student groups historically underrepresented in CS.

Education Development Center is recruiting high schools to participate in an exciting 2.5 year school-based program centered on the use of BJC, and funded by the U.S. Department of Education, which includes the following:

  1. School CS planning. Develop a school-based plan to increase participation in AP CSP.
  2. Teacher online professional development. Participate in a week-long BJC PD in summer 2022 (multiple opportunities available starting June 27th) and a school-year professional learning community (estimated 5–10 hours of time) in 2022–23 and 2023–24 for a total stipend of $1000.
  3. BJC student and teacher curriculum resources are all free through bjc.edc.org.

Who should participate?

Teacher Perspectives

  • “The students love it.”
  • “Engaging, relevant, and somewhat addictive.”
  • “The teacher resources are phenomenal.”
  • “Wonderful and very thought-out.”

High schools interested in participating

  1. Want to increase their AP CSP participation and success
  2. Want to offer students an engaging way to learn about CS and explore the social implications of technology
  3. Serve a significant proportion of students from low-income families
  4. Have significant populations of students typically underrepresented in CS

What are the requirements for participation?

  • The creation of a school team that includes at least one teacher who will use BJC in an AP CSP course in 2022–23 and 2023–24; any other CS teachers; a school administrator; a STEM, CS, or math coordinator/department chair; and/or a guidance counselor.
  • Team participation in three consultations/year with BJC staff, beginning March 2022.
  • Teacher participation in PD and commitment to using BJC for two years.
  • Completion of teacher and school team surveys; teacher participation in focus groups.
  • Administrative support of data requests to the district for anonymized student data.

======= Recruiting High Schools for AP Computer Science Principles

BJC School Equity Program

The BJC School Equity Program will provide schools with a research-based school support strategy that will help establish or expand AP Computer Science Principles (AP CSP) course offerings using the Beauty and Joy of Computing (BJC) curriculum.

Apply to the BJC School Equity Program!

Benefits

  • Free AP CSP student and teacher curriculum materials
  • A $1000 stipend for each participating teacher
  • Fifty hours of online PD during the summer and throughout the school year
  • Consultations to increase student participation and success

School Eligibility

  • Public high school in the US
  • Serving students from low-income families
  • Serving students from groups typically underrepresented in CS including girls, Black, and Hispanic or Latinx students

School and Teacher Commitment

  • Offer a BJC AP CSP course in 2023–24 and 2024–25
  • Teacher participation in BJC summer 2023 PD
  • Teacher participation in twice-yearly consultations with BJC staff
  • School and teacher completion of surveys
  • District responds to anonymized student data requests

About BJC

Beauty and Joy of Computing is a College Board-endorsed, AP CSP curriculum designed to provide students with a rigorous and engaging introduction to CS and to expand access and participation in CS for all students, with an emphasis on student groups historically underrepresented in CS. BJC uses the blocks-based programming language Snap!, which offers a low-threshold of entry yet a high-ceiling of powerful programming capacity. BJC provides a robust set of teacher supports as well as an active online teacher community.

About this Project

Education Development Center (EDC) has received funding from the U.S. Department of Education to research and develop the BJC School Equity Program centered on the implementation of the Beauty and Joy of Computing curriculum. June Mark is the Principal Investigator.
