The BJC School Equity Program will provide schools with a research-based school support strategy that will help establish or expand AP Computer Science Principles (AP CSP) course offerings using the Beauty and Joy of Computing (BJC) curriculum.

We invite you to join and support our research efforts!

Benefits

  • A $1000 stipend for each participating AP CSP teacher.
  • Approximately 50 hours of College Board Endorsed teacher online PD.
  • BJC student and teacher curriculum resources - all free through bjc.edc.org.
  • School CS planning resources and guidance to increase student participation and success in AP CSP.

Eligibility

  • Be a public high school in the United States.
  • Want to offer students an engaging way to learn about CS and explore the social implications of technology.
  • Serve a significant proportion of students from low-income families or from groups that are typically underrepresented in CS.

Commitment

  • Offer an AP CSP course using the BJC curriculum during the 2023–24 and 2024–25 school years.
  • Teacher participation in BJC PD during the summer of 2023.
  • Participation in two check-ins a year with BJC staff.
  • Completion of surveys by the teacher and other supporting staff members.
  • Administrative support of data requests to the district for anonymized student data.

More Information

The Education Development Center (EDC) has received funding from the U.S. Department of Education to implement a five-year project to refine and study the impact of the Improving Equity in AP CS Principles program, a two-year computer science equity program centered on the implementation of the Beauty and Joy of Computing (BJC) curriculum. June Mark, jmark@edc.org, is the Principal Investigator.

The Improving Equity in AP CSP program will provide participating schools with robust school and teacher supports to strengthen implementation of BJC and help ensure that high-need students have access to equitable CS instruction. BJC is a College Board-endorsed AP CSP course designed to provide students with a rigorous and engaging introduction to CS and to expand access and participation in CS for all students, with an emphasis on student groups historically underrepresented in CS.
