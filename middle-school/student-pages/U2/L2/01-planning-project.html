Planning Your Project

In this activity, you will think about the kind of interactive project you want to create.

You'll create characters or objects that move around (sprites) on top of a background image (the Snap! stage), and you'll control how they look by changing their costumes.

  • The stage is the big white rectangle in the upper right corner of your Snap! window. It's what users of your project see, and it's where your characters and objects (your sprites) live.
  • A sprite is like an actor on the stage who can do many different things, such as walk, talk, and whatever else you program it to do!
  • A costume is a picture that can be "worn" by a sprite.

New Snap! Skills

You're going to create an interactive project (such as a poster, a card, or a cartoon) using each of the following new skills you will build:

  1. Costumes: You can use the ones built-in to Snap!, import pictures from the Internet, or draw your own.
  2. Animation: You'll make your sprites move around the stage, say things, and change costumes.
  3. Importing Code: You'll incorporate blocks from prior projects.
  4. Sounds: You'll add or create audio recordings.
  5. Events: You'll make your code respond to user clicks (such as on a sprite) by playing a sound, moving, or writing something for the user to read.
  6. Hot Spots: You'll create special areas on the stage where clicking produces a special effect.

The Stage is what users of your project see. One or more sprites (characters) live on and can talk and move around on the stage. Sprites begin with an arrowhead costume (as shown in the picture of the Snap! interface), but you can change a sprite's costume (the picture that it wears) by choosing "Costumes..." the Snap! File menu ().
