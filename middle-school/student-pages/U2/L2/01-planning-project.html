Planning Your Project

In this activity, you plan to develop an interactive Snap! project.

You'll create characters or objects that move around (sprites) on top of a background image (the Snap! stage). You'll control how the sprites look by changing their costumes, and you'll control how the stage looks by changing its background.

  • The stage is the rectangle in the upper right of the Snap! window. It's what users of your project see, and it's where your sprites live.
  • A sprite is like an actor on the stage who can do many different things, such as walk, talk, and whatever else you program it to do!
  • A costume is a picture that can be "worn" by a sprite.
  • A background is a picture that can be "worn" by the stage.
You first learned about sprites, costumes, and the Snap! stage in Unit 1 Lab 4 Activity 3: Investigating and Storing Images.

New Snap! Skills

You're going to create an interactive project (such as a poster, a card, or a cartoon) using each of the following new skills you will build in this lab:

  1. Costumes and Backgrounds: You can use the ones built-in to Snap!, import pictures from the Internet, or draw your own.
  2. Animation: You'll make your sprites move around the stage, say things, and change costumes.
  3. Importing Code: You'll incorporate blocks from prior projects.
  4. Sounds: You'll add or create audio recordings.
  5. Events: You'll make your code perform certain actions (such as playing a sound or moving) when certain things happen (such as when the user clicks a sprite or when something else has finished happening).
  6. Hot Spots: You'll create special areas on the stage where the user can click to produce an specific action.
