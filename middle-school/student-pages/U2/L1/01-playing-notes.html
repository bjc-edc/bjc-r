In this activity, you'll teach the computer to play some simple songs and move those songs up and down the musical scale.

    2. A command block tells the computer to do something without reporting a value.

  2. Open this Song Player project, and save it to your account. It includes a command block that takes two inputs: a note to play and how many beats to play it. Change the inputs to the play note block, and then click it to see how the note changes.

    3. The note can be any whole number from 0 to 127, where 60 represents the note middle C. If you click the triangle, a little piano menu will open, and you can hear the note you are selecting.

    The number of beats tells the computer how long to play that note. The values work just like in music: 1 beat is a whole note, 0.5 beats is a half note, 0.25 beats is a quarter note, etc.

    Connecting several blocks together one after another is called sequencing.

  3. Attach several play note blocks together one after another to begin creating a song.
    • You can use to create breaks in your song.
    • You can use block to repeat any command (or group of commands) a specific number of times.
    • You can also use to change the instrument, to change the tempo (pace), or to change the volume. Wherever you put one of these blocks, the sound will change from that point on.
