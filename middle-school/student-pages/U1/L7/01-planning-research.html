Designing Your Research Plan

In Unit 1 Lab 3: Dealing with Data, you created a survey with one question and created a pictograph of the results. You've learned a lot since then, and you are now prepared to create a survey with multiple questions and to use Snap! to process the results for you.

    Make a little worksheet to go along with this.
  1. Decide on a research question that you can investigate by surveying your classmates on a topic that interests you.
    For everyone's privacy, don't choose a research question that will require asking for identifying information that would tell you who answered what on the survey such as name, address, phone number, birthday, student ID number, Snap! username, etc.

    Since Lab 3, you have developed stronger Snap! skills; so you can research a topic that will require multiple survey questions to investigate and you can use Snap! to do math for you. Here are some ideas:

    • How many favorite musical artist do people have?
    • How long have people practiced their favorite sports?
    • Do students do more extracurricular activities at certain age?
    • How long does it take people to get to school?

    2. Remember, your survey should be anonymous; it should not collect any personal information.
  2. Design a survey to answer your question. Your survey should:
    1. Have more than one question
    2. Take numbers as responses to at least one question
