In this activity, you will choose a research question and design and set up your survey.

You've learned a lot since then, and you can now research a topic that will require multiple survey questions to investigate how two (or more) variables are related. And you can use Snap! to process the results for you.

For privacy, don't design a research plan that requires asking for personal information such as name, address, phone number, birthday, etc.
  1. Design a research question to investigate the relationship between two (or more) variables, and design your survey questions. Your survey should:
    1. Have one question to collect data about each variable you are investigating
    2. Take numbers as responses to at least one question

    Click for example research questions, variables, and survey questions.

    Example Research Question: Do people stick with some sports longer than other sports?

    • Possible Variables: sport played, years spent playing sport
    • Possible Survey Questions:
      • What sport do you practice most?
      • How many years have you been practicing that sport?

    Example Research Question: Do people who have practiced music longer listen to more music?

    • Possible Variables: years spent playing music, hours spent listening to music each day
    • Possible Survey Questions:
      • How many years have you been playing music?
      • How many hours do you listen to music each day?

  1. Come up with an overall strategy for using those Snap! blocks to investigate your question. If you realize your survey questions can't be researched using those blocks, you should redesign your survey before you go on.

    Click for example strategies for investigating a question.

    Example Research Question: Do people stick with some sports longer than other sports?
    Example Variables: sport played, years spent playing sport

    • Possible data processing strategy: Make Snap! process the data for each sport played one at a time and find the average years spent playing each one.

    Example Research Question: Do people who have practiced music longer listen to more music? Example Variables: years spent playing music, hours spent listening to music each day

    • Possible data processing strategy: Make Snap! identify the maximum, minimum, and average number of years spent playing music, use that to choose a cutoff value (such as three years of playing music), and then have Snap! find the average hours spent listening for people who have played more than or less than that cutoff value and compare.
