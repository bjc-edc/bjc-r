Saying Hi

When you open the project, you'll see this single block in the Scripts area (the central space of the Snap! window):

  1. Type your name in the blank slot, and click the block to see what happens. Did you see a bubble pop-up?

Every Snap! program is made up of blocks, and those blocks tell the computer what to do. The job of this block is to say hello to the name entered.

  1. Choose a different language from the dropdown that says "English" and click the block again. What changed about the bubble this time?

You just saw how changing the inputs to a block changed its output. Now let's introduce some terminology.

: Reporters and Inputs

  • There are multiple types of blocks in Snap. This say hi block is a reporter. That means that it does some sort of computation and then "reports" the result to the rest of the program. When you click the block, you see the reported result in that bubble, like this:

    There can be infinitely many reporter blocks in the universe, since there are so many things that we can compute. This particular block reports a personal greeting, by combining your name with a translation of the word "hello" in the specific language. The output of this block is text, but other blocks might output numbers, lists, tables, or even images.

  • Many blocks accept inputs that customize what they compute. The "say hi to" block accepts two inputs, your name and a language. If it didn't know your name, then it would only be able to translate a greeting, not personalize it. üòü The more inputs a block accepts, the more its output can be customized.

    If you've ever been to a sandwich shop and answered questions like "what kind of cheese?", "what kind of sauce?", "extra toppings?", it's a lot like that. The more questions the shop asks you, the more you get to customize your sandwich!
