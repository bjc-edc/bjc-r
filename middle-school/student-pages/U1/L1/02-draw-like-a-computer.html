Draw Like a Computer

In this activity, you will take turns pretending to be a programmer (whose job it is to give drawing instructions), and a computer (whose job it is to follow the instructions precisely, but try to deviate from what is probably intended to be drawn).

    Imagine the computer (here, a person) only knows how to draw:
    • a straight line
    • a polygon (triangle, square, pentagon, etc.)
    • a circle
    • an arc (a part of a circle)
    You can tell the computer how big to make things (e.g., 2 inches long) and at what angle things should be positioned (e.g., at a 45° angle).
  1. Using no more than a handful of the basic drawing elements from the list to the right (line, polygon, circle, and arc), create a simple drawing that you want the computer to draw, and don't let anyone see it...
  2. Then write a list of instructions for re-creating your drawing, being as precise as possible.
  3. With a partner, take turns being the programmer and being the computer:
    • The programmer should provide the instructions (and only the instructionsâ€”no extra help or hints about what the drawing should look like) to the computer. Don't peek at what the drawing looks like until the end!
    • The computer should follow the instructions as precisely as possible, deliberately trying to take things very literally.
  4. Then, share the original drawings and compare them to the computer's interpretation. 
    • If the drawings match, the programmer has "won". 
    • If the drawings don't match at all, the computer has "won". 
  5. What instructions could the programmer have given more precisely?
