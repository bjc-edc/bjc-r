Pet Inventor

In this activity, you'll start to make an interactive, imaginary creature that uses the micro:bit LED display.

Collect Materials

For each activity, we'll provide you with a list of materials to gather and some suggested materials.

  • micro:bit
  • USB cable
  • Battery pack (optional)
  • General craft supplies
    • Scissors
    • Markers
    • Glue stick
    • Colorful paper
    • Carboard
    • Pencils
    • Decorative materials (googly eyes, pipe cleaners, etc)

Get Started

Imagine a pet that you would like to create. This can be a real, fictional, or entirely new creature. Your pet will respond to your touch using micro:bit’s sensors and inputs, and the LED display will add to your creature’s personality. Think about how it spends its day or activities it enjoys doing.

  1. Brainstorm what creature you would like invent. Use a pencil and sketch what it will look like
  2. Think first on your own and then discuss with a partner:
    • What will its body look like? What materials do you need?
    • Where will the micro:bit go? How will it be used to enhance the pet?
    • What will be shown on the LED display?
  3. Your pet will also become interactive in (at least) one of these ways: press a button, tilt the micro:bit, or change in light level (see L1: Meet micro:bit). Start thinking about how to incorporate on of these inputs into your interactive pet.

Once you have a pet in mind, start building it with the suggested supplies as well as other available materials. When ready, start making your interactive component.

In this activity, you decided on a pet to create and how to make it interactive using micro:bit.
