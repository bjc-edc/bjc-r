Create a Face

In this activity, you'll design your own face to appear on your micro:bit in MicroBlocks. Then, you can experiment with making your face interactive using the light sensor.

Animate Your Face

Bring your face to life by adding a second display with another expression.

Tip: Animations are a rapid sequence of images displayed sequentially. To bring your face to life, you'll need to switch between display blocks quickly, but not too quickly. To achieve this, use a wait block after each display. Play with the amount of time until you find one that you like.

  1. Make a second expression using another display block.
  2. Create a loop to cycle through both expressions.

Make an Interactive Face

So far, your face changes on its own, but you can control when it changes between faces. One way to do this is to use the light sensor on the micro:bit. It can detect different levels of light, so you can change your micro:bit face whether its bright or dark (by covering the micro:bit with your hand).

Tip: Light levels vary greatly depending on the room that you're in, so you'll need to experiment with values for what's considered bright or dark. One way to do this is to use the say block. Click for a picture.
  1. Use an if statement and light levelto change the micro:bit's facial expression.
