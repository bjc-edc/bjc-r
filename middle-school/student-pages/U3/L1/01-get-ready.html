Get Ready

In this activity, you'll set up your micro:bit and get write your first program in MicroBlocks.

Collect Materials

For each activity, we'll provide you with a list of materials to gather and some suggested materials.

  • micro:bit
  • USB cable
  • Battery pack (optional)

Set Up MicroBlocks

For this unit, you'll use a program called MicroBlocks, a Snap! programming language designed to control tools like micro:bit.

  1. Open MicroBlocks in either a Chrome, Firefox, Edge browser.
  2. Connect your micro:bit to your computer with a USB cable. Select your board from the browser dialog box, then click Connect.
  3. A green circle will automatically appear behind the USB icon, showing that the micro:bit is connected. If not, ask your teacher for help.

Your micro:bit is now connected, but you'll need to add more blocks to program it. You'll do this by adding a couple of libraries to MicroBlocks.

  1. Click the + symbol next to Libraries.
  2. Select Basic Sensors.ubl and then click open. Do the same for LED Display.ubl.

One block that's unique to micro:bit is the display block. It's designed to look like the grid of LEDs on one side of the board. There are 25 LEDs that you can turn on and off individually.

  1. Drag the display block into the Scripts area. Design your own face by clicking the LED rectangles to turn them on or off.
  2. Click on the block once and a smiley face will appear on your micro:bit.
In this activity, you learned how to set up a coding environment for micro:bit and made a face display on the micro:bit LED display.
